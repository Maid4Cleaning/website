10 Quick and Easy Chores You Can Do in 30 Seconds or Less

If you’ve ever put off chores because they feel too time-consuming, you’re not alone! While deep cleaning projects like organizing your closet or scrubbing the fridge take a while, plenty of small tasks can be completed in just 30 seconds. These quick chores make a big difference in keeping your home fresh and tidy, and they help prevent messes from piling up.

Here are ten easy cleaning tasks you can knock out in under a minute!

1. Wipe Down Kitchen Surfaces

Your kitchen sees a lot of action, from food prep to family meals. Keeping surfaces clean helps maintain a hygienic space and prevents grime buildup. Grab a microfiber cloth and a gentle cleaner to tackle:

  • Kitchen countertops
  • Refrigerator handles
  • Kitchen sink
  • Faucets and knobs
  • Stovetop crumbs
  • Microwave door and handle
  • Cabinet fronts and drawer handles
  • Light switches and doorknobs

Pro Tip: Use a damp microfiber cloth with warm water and a drop of dish soap for a streak-free shine. Avoid using vinegar on stone countertops like granite or marble, as it can cause damage.

2. Empty Small Waste Bins

Taking out the trash doesn’t need to be a big task! Tie up the garbage bag and replace it with a fresh liner. To make this even quicker, store spare bin liners at the bottom of your wastebasket. Sprinkle a little baking soda at the base of your trash can to neutralize odors before inserting a new bag.

3. Squeegee Shower Walls

Prevent soap scum buildup and water spots by running a squeegee over your shower walls after each use. This simple step reduces the frequency of deep cleaning and keeps your bathroom looking fresh.

Extra Tip: Spray your shower with a daily cleaning solution (equal parts water and isopropyl alcohol) to cut down on soap residue and mildew growth.

4. Shine Bathroom Mirrors

A clean mirror brightens up your bathroom instantly. Spray a small amount of glass cleaner or a diluted vinegar solution onto a microfiber cloth (never directly on the mirror to prevent streaking). Wipe the mirror in a circular motion for a streak-free finish.

5. Refresh Your Pet’s Water Bowl

Your furry friends deserve fresh water! Give their bowl a quick rinse and refill it with clean water. If you have 30 extra seconds, give it a light scrub with a bit of dish soap to remove slimy buildup.

Extra Tip: Set a reminder to fully sanitize food and water bowls at least once a week to keep bacteria at bay.

6. Vacuum the Entryway

Your entryway collects dirt, leaves, and dust from foot traffic throughout the day. A quick pass with a handheld or stick vacuum prevents debris from spreading into the rest of the house.

Pro Tip: Use a doormat inside and outside your door to trap dirt before it enters your home.

7. Clean Your Touchscreens

From smartphones to tablets, touchscreens can accumulate fingerprints and bacteria. Power down your device and gently wipe it with a slightly damp microfiber cloth. Avoid using household cleaners, as they can damage the screen coating.

Extra Tip: Use a 70% isopropyl alcohol wipe for deeper cleaning, especially during flu season.

8. Sort Through Junk Mail

Junk mail can clutter up countertops quickly. Take 30 seconds to toss unwanted letters, flyers, and ads straight into the recycling bin. Consider keeping a designated spot for incoming mail to avoid future pileups.

9. Dust a Small Surface

Grab a microfiber duster and quickly wipe down a side table, bookshelf, or nightstand. Regular dusting prevents buildup and improves indoor air quality.

Extra Tip: Dust from top to bottom to avoid knocking particles onto freshly cleaned areas.

10. Fluff and Rotate Throw Pillows

Give your living room an instant refresh by fluffing up your throw pillows and rotating them to maintain their shape. If you have extra time, lightly mist them with a fabric refresher.

Making the Most of Quick Cleaning Tasks

Once you realize how much you can get done in just 30 seconds, you’ll start spotting more ways to keep your home tidy without spending hours cleaning. Small, consistent efforts lead to a more organized, stress-free home.
