10 Things You Should Always Clean Every Day (Without Question)

Life can get messy fast, especially with kids, pets, family gatherings, and everyday living. Regular cleaning isn’t just about appearances—it helps prevent the spread of harmful bacteria, allergens, and grime that accumulate throughout the day. Keeping your home clean daily makes deep cleaning less overwhelming and creates a healthier space for your family.

As the go-to cleaning experts in St. Louis, Maid 4 Cleaning knows exactly what needs daily attention to keep your home fresh and germ-free. Here are the top 10 things you should clean every day—without question.

1. Kitchen Counters

Your kitchen counters see a lot of action, from food prep to mail drop-offs. Use a disinfectant spray or hot soapy water and a microfiber cloth to wipe them down after every meal to remove bacteria, crumbs, and spills.

2. Bathroom Counters and Sinks

Bathroom counters accumulate soap scum, water spots, and bacteria from daily grooming. Wipe them down with a disinfectant or a solution of hydrogen peroxide and water. Don’t forget to clean the sink to prevent toothpaste residue and water stains.

3. Dishes and Sink

Leaving dirty dishes in the sink overnight creates a breeding ground for bacteria and attracts pests. Wash dishes immediately after use or run the dishwasher daily. Don’t forget to wipe down your sink with dish soap or a vinegar-water mix (never mix vinegar and baking soda, as they neutralize each other).

4. Sponges and Dishcloths

Sponges are a haven for bacteria due to their moisture retention. Clean your sponge daily by microwaving it for 60 seconds (ensure it's damp to prevent fire hazards) or soaking it in a solution of hot water and dish soap. Consider replacing sponges every 1-2 weeks to maintain hygiene.

5. Phone Screen

Your phone goes everywhere with you, making it a prime spot for bacteria. Wipe it down daily with an alcohol-based wipe or a microfiber cloth lightly dampened with a screen-safe cleaner.

6. Computer Keyboard and Mouse

Your keyboard contains 400 times more bacteria than a toilet seat! Use an electronic-safe disinfectant wipe or isopropyl alcohol on a microfiber cloth to clean your keyboard and mouse daily, especially if multiple people use the computer.

7. Remote Controls

Remotes are one of the dirtiest items in your home. Wipe them down daily with a disinfecting wipe or an alcohol-soaked cotton pad, making sure to get between buttons where grime accumulates.

8. Dining Room Table

After meals, wipe down your dining table with a damp cloth and a mild cleaner. For specific surfaces:

  • Wood: Use a microfiber cloth with warm water and dry immediately.

  • Glass: A microfiber cloth with a vinegar-water solution works wonders.

  • Metal: Wipe with warm water and mild dish soap, avoiding abrasive pads.

9. Keys

House and car keys are handled frequently but rarely cleaned. Wash them weekly with warm water and soap, or wipe them down daily with a disinfectant wipe.

10. Toothbrush

A damp toothbrush left in the bathroom can harbor bacteria. Rinse it thoroughly after each use and let it air dry upright. To disinfect, soak in a 3% hydrogen peroxide solution for 15 minutes (longer can damage bristles). Replace your toothbrush every 3 months.
