13 Surprising Things You Can Clean with Glass Cleaner

Most homes in Saint Louis have a trusty bottle of glass cleaner under the sink—and while it’s perfect for getting windows and mirrors crystal clear, that’s just the beginning of what it can do. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we’re big fans of turning everyday items into powerful multi-purpose tools. Glass cleaner? Total MVP.

Keep in mind: most store-bought glass cleaners contain ammonia, which makes them effective degreasers—but also means you should never use them on your skin, pets, or electronic screens. Now let’s get into all the surprising ways you can put that bottle to work.

1. Tackle Grease in the Kitchen

Use glass cleaner to cut through stubborn kitchen grease on stovetops, backsplashes, and even cabinet doors. Spray it on, let it sit for a few minutes, then wipe clean with a soft cloth. Rinse any surface that touches food.

2. Wipe Down Countertops

Glass cleaner works on sealed granite, quartz, laminate, and stainless steel. It leaves a nice shine without streaks—just avoid unsealed natural stone, and give food-prep surfaces a water rinse after.

3. Remove Sticky Gunk

Stickers and labels can leave a sticky mess. Spray a bit of glass cleaner on the area, let it soak in, and wipe off. Great for glass jars, metal tools, and even your car’s bumper.

4. Deep-Clean Bathroom Surfaces

Beyond mirrors, glass cleaner is effective on tile, sinks, tubs, and even toilets. Spray it into the toilet bowl to help remove stains, or use it to shine up faucets and glass shower doors.

5. Spot-Clean Microfiber Upholstery

Mist lightly on microfiber to lift small stains without leaving water marks. Use a soft-bristled brush and let the fabric dry completely. Always test first on a hidden spot.

6. Sanitize Toys the Fast Way

Plastic toys get sticky fast. Spray with glass cleaner, wipe with a cloth, then rinse with water. It’s a quick fix that works for blocks, dolls, and more.

7. Shine Up Faucets & Handles

Glass cleaner quickly removes water spots, fingerprints, and grime from doorknobs, handles, and metal fixtures. Perfect for making things sparkle before guests arrive.

8. Brighten Jewelry

Give your rings and chains a quick shine by spraying with glass cleaner and scrubbing gently with a soft toothbrush. Rinse thoroughly. (Skip this on porous stones like pearls or turquoise.)

9. Pre-Treat Laundry Stains

Spray laundry stains with colorless glass cleaner and let sit for 10–15 minutes before washing. Works especially well on greasy or red stains. Test delicates first.

10. Clean Carpet Spots

Blot carpet stains with a cloth dampened with glass cleaner. Repeat as needed—no need for expensive stain removers. Avoid soaking the carpet.

11. Get Rid of Fabric & Plastic Odors

Odors in containers, bags, or upholstery? Mist the area lightly, let it air dry, and enjoy the freshness. (Again, colorless formulas are best.)

12. Detail Your Car’s Interior

Use glass cleaner on dashboards, center consoles, door panels, and steering wheels. Spray onto a microfiber cloth—not directly on surfaces—and avoid tinted windows.

13. Freshen Patio Furniture

Glass cleaner is a great way to keep your outdoor furniture looking clean and polished. Spray and wipe regularly to prevent grime buildup.
