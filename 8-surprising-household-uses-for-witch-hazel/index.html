8 Surprising Household Uses for Witch Hazel

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we believe a clean home starts with a few great staples—and witch hazel is one of those underrated gems hiding in your medicine cabinet. Known for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties, it’s a skincare favorite, but did you know it’s also a handy helper around the house?

Whether you live in a cozy apartment in the Central West End or a family home in Webster Groves, these clever uses for witch hazel can freshen up your space—naturally.

A Quick Note First

Witch hazel is not a disinfectant. While it offers skin-soothing benefits, it won’t eliminate viruses or bacteria like bleach, hydrogen peroxide, or isopropyl alcohol. For true disinfection, stick with EPA-approved cleaners—or leave it to the pros. Maid 4 Cleaning proudly offers enhanced disinfection services using hospital-grade products. 1. Wipe Down Window Blinds with Ease

Blinds collect dust, grime, and in the kitchen—grease. Witch hazel can cut through buildup gently without damaging materials.

DIY Blind Cleaner:

  • 1 cup witch hazel

  • 1 cup water

  • A drop of gentle dish soap

Spray onto a microfiber cloth and wipe each slat. For deeper grime, mist directly onto the blinds and follow with a dry cloth.

2. Shine Chrome and Glass Fixtures

Skip the streaks! Witch hazel dries fast, making it perfect for polishing bathroom faucets, mirrors, and glass surfaces. Spray undiluted witch hazel and wipe clean with a lint-free cloth.

3. Mop High-Shine Floors

Vinyl, tile, and linoleum love this natural shine booster:

  • ½ cup witch hazel

  • 1 gallon warm water

  • Optional: 1 tablespoon borax for tough grime

It lifts residue without leaving dull streaks. Perfect for regular upkeep in busy Saint Louis kitchens and bathrooms.

4. Deodorize Yoga Mats and Home Gym Gear

Mix equal parts witch hazel and distilled water in a spray bottle, then add 10 drops of tea tree oil. After your workout, spritz your mat or gear and let it air dry. It’s gentle on skin, powerful on funk!

5. Gently Clean Jewelry

Use a cotton pad dampened with witch hazel to remove skin oils and debris from rings, earrings, and chains. For a deeper clean, soak pieces for 20–30 minutes, rinse, and dry with a soft cloth.

6. DIY Room Spray That Actually Works

Create a signature scent for your home:

  • 1 part witch hazel

  • 1 part distilled water

  • 10–30 drops of essential oils (lavender, eucalyptus, citrus)

Witch hazel helps oils blend and boosts their staying power. Spritz around rooms, linens, or closets for a quick refresh.

7. Lift Set-In Blood Stains from Fabric

This one’s surprising but effective:

  • Soak the stained fabric area in witch hazel for 30 minutes

  • Rinse with cold water

  • Wash as usual

It helps break down proteins in dried blood, saving your favorite shirt or pillowcase.

8. Remove Sticky Residue

Stubborn sticker goo on glassware or price tag residue? Soak a cotton pad in witch hazel, press it onto the spot for a few minutes, then wipe or gently scrape with a plastic card.
