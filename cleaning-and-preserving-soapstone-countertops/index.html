The Ultimate Guide to Cleaning and Preserving Soapstone Countertops

Soapstone countertops are a stylish and durable choice for kitchens and bathrooms. With their beautiful appearance, heat resistance, and stain-fighting properties, it's easy to see why soapstone is becoming a popular option among homeowners. However, proper cleaning and maintenance are key to preserving their pristine look. Maid 4 Cleaning has put together expert advice to help you maintain your soapstone countertops effortlessly.

Why Choose Soapstone Countertops?

Soapstone countertops offer several advantages over other materials:

  • Non-Porous Surface: Soapstone doesn't absorb liquids, making stains less likely.

  • Durability: It resists heat and everyday scratches.

  • Easy Maintenance: Scratches can be sanded away easily, ensuring a long-lasting, elegant appearance.

Safe and Effective Cleaning Methods for Soapstone

Here are our recommended methods for safely maintaining your soapstone countertops:

1. Daily Cleaning with Gentle Soap and Water
Mix warm water with mild dish soap to create a gentle cleaning solution. Clean the surface with a soft microfiber cloth, rinse thoroughly, and dry immediately to prevent water spots.

Important: Avoid acidic or abrasive cleaning solutions like vinegar or lemon juice, as these can dull the surface over time.

Monthly Maintenance with Mineral Oil

Soapstone naturally develops a dark, rich patina with regular use of mineral oil. To maintain the countertop’s beautiful finish:

  • Apply a thin layer of food-grade mineral oil once a month.

  • Allow it to soak in for about 30 minutes.

  • Wipe off any excess with a soft, dry cloth.

Quick Tips for Long-Lasting Soapstone Countertops

  • Immediately clean up spills to maintain the soapstone’s appearance.

  • Always use gentle, non-abrasive cloths or sponges.

  • Periodically buff out minor scratches using fine sandpaper.
