Cleaning Tips for Allergy Sufferers in Saint Louis

Living with environmental allergies in Saint Louis can feel like a constant battle—especially during peak pollen season. But while staying inside may help, letting allergens build up indoors only makes symptoms worse. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we understand that clean doesn’t just mean tidy—it means healthy. If you or a loved one suffers from allergies, here’s how to keep your home allergen-free without triggering a sneezing fit.

1. Set a Consistent Cleaning Routine

A solid routine is key. Allergens like dust mites, pet dander, and pollen settle in fast. Instead of waiting for things to get bad, aim to do a little each day. Schedule tasks throughout the week—think of it as maintenance, not a marathon.

Pro tip: Clean during low-pollen times, like midday or after a rainstorm, and keep windows closed while working.

2. Vacuum the Right Way (and Often)

Vacuuming is one of the most effective ways to reduce allergens—but only if your vacuum is up to the job. Choose a model with a true HEPA filter. According to the American Academy of Allergy, Asthma & Immunology, HEPA filters trap fine particles like pollen, pet dander, and dust mites.

Vacuum tips:

  • Do carpets, rugs, and upholstered furniture at least twice a week.

  • Don’t forget under furniture and along baseboards.

  • Use a vacuum attachment to get into tight corners.

3. Dust Smart, Not Hard

Skip feather dusters or dry cloths—they just spread allergens around. Use a slightly damp microfiber cloth to trap and remove dust. Martha Stewart’s Homekeeping Handbook recommends microfiber for its ability to cling to dust without releasing it back into the air.

4. Wash Bedding Weekly

Your bed is a hotspot for allergens. Wash all sheets, pillowcases, and blankets weekly in hot water (at least 130°F) using fragrance-free, dye-free detergent. Vacuum your mattress and under the bed every two weeks.

Bonus: Use allergy-proof covers on mattresses and pillows.

5. Don’t Forget Curtains, Blinds, and Ceiling Fans

Soft furnishings collect dust quickly. Wash machine-safe curtains monthly, or vacuum them using an upholstery attachment. Wipe blinds with a microfiber blind cleaner. Ceiling fans should be cleaned weekly—dust on fan blades can get circulated throughout your room.

6. Keep Bathroom Humidity Low

Mold is a common allergen, and bathrooms are its favorite hangout. After showers, run an exhaust fan or open the door to reduce moisture. Wipe down walls and floors with a microfiber cloth. Don’t forget to clean behind and around the toilet, where dust and hair often collect.

Prevent mold with:

  • A squeegee for shower walls

  • Mold-resistant shower liners

  • Regular checks under sinks for leaks

7. Purify Your Air

Saint Louis allergy seasons can be rough, so it’s worth investing in air quality:

  • HEPA air purifiers are great for bedrooms and living rooms.

  • Change HVAC filters regularly (every 30–90 days).

  • Keep houseplants in check—water them with ice cubes to avoid pooling moisture that can lead to mold.

8. Declutter to Defend Against Dust

Clutter creates more surfaces for allergens to collect. According to Decluttering and Organizing Your Home by Jessica Allen, simplifying your home reduces cleaning time and improves mental clarity. Less stuff = fewer allergens.

9. Skip the Scents

Fragrances in candles, air fresheners, and cleaning products can irritate sensitive airways. Stick with unscented, allergy-friendly products. If you're unsure, we at Maid 4 Cleaning can help you choose safer options.
