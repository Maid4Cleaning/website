Common-Sense Ways to Deep Clean and Disinfect Your Home

Keeping your home fresh, clean, and safe doesn’t have to feel overwhelming. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we believe a little common sense and a solid plan can make all the difference. Whether you’re deep cleaning your home in the Saint Louis area or simply freshening up between professional cleanings, here’s how to tackle germs and grime the smart way.

Cleaning vs. Disinfecting: Know the Difference

Understanding the cleaning lingo can help you use products correctly and make your efforts more effective:

  • Cleaning removes visible dirt, crumbs, and dust—usually with soap or detergent and water.

  • Disinfecting uses chemicals to kill most germs on surfaces.

  • Sanitizing reduces germs to a safe level, as defined by public health standards.

  • Sterilizing kills all microbes, but this level of cleanliness is generally reserved for hospitals.

When you clean, you’re removing germs. When you disinfect, you’re killing them. For best results? Do both.

How to Properly Clean and Disinfect Surfaces

  1. Start with a clean surface. Use a microfiber cloth with soapy water to wipe away dirt and grease.

  2. Apply your disinfectant. Let it sit for the contact time listed on the label—typically 30 seconds to 10 minutes.

  3. Let it air dry. Don’t rush the drying process—it’s key to effectiveness.

  4. Rinse if needed. Especially on food-contact surfaces like cutting boards or countertops.

💡 Tip from the pros: Microfiber cloths trap dirt and germs better than cotton and can be washed and reused hundreds of times​.

DIY Disinfectant Spray (For Non-Porous Surfaces)

  • Mix 1 tablespoon of chlorine bleach with 1 quart of water.

  • Apply with a spray bottle.

  • Let sit for 10 minutes before wiping or rinsing.

  • Make a fresh batch daily.

Never mix bleach with anything but water, and be sure to use gloves and ventilate the space well​.

Saint Louis’ Most Forgotten Germ Hot Spots

Some surfaces in our homes get touched often—but cleaned rarely. Add these to your weekly checklist:

  • Light switches & door handles

  • Thermostats & stair rails

  • Remote controls & game controllers

  • Refrigerator handles

  • Cell phones & tablets

  • Keys and wallets

💡 Pro Tip: Use a disinfectant wipe or a spritz on a microfiber cloth (never directly on electronics).

Go Beyond the Basics: Clean with Confidence

Deep cleaning isn’t just about disinfecting. It’s about refreshing your entire space.

  • Kitchen: Deodorize garbage disposals with citrus peels and hot water. Clean behind appliances at least once a season.

  • Bathroom: Let cleaner sit on grout or tile for at least 10 minutes before scrubbing.

  • Floors: Vacuum before you mop to remove dry debris. Use two buckets—one for clean water and one to rinse your mop—to avoid spreading dirt.

  • Upholstery & mattresses: Use a disinfectant fabric spray or steam clean seasonally.
