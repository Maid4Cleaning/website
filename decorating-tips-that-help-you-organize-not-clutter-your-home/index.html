Decorating Tips That Help You Organize — Not Clutter — Your Home

Decorating your home is a fun and personal journey—choosing color palettes, curating artwork, and finding that perfect cozy throw. But here’s a little-known secret: some of those décor choices might actually be making your home look cluttered. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we help Saint Louis homes shine—literally and visually. Here’s how to keep your home stylish and serene.

1. Start with a Decluttering Detox

Before you dive into decorating, take a good look around. If you’re displaying five family photo frames on one mantle, ask yourself—do they all need to be there? A less-is-more approach gives your special pieces space to breathe. According to Jessica Allen’s guide on decluttering, the number one cause of visual chaos is simply having too much out on display.

💡 Pro Tip: If it hasn’t been used, admired, or cleaned in a year, donate or store it.

2. Style in Groups, Not Scatters

Randomly spaced objects—one vase here, a candle there—can make a room feel messy. Instead, group similar items by type, size, or material. Nestle candles on a small tray or stack coordinating books beneath a decorative object.

This technique helps the eye rest, not dart around the room.

3. Rethink the Bookshelf

Bookshelves should show off your style, not your storage crisis. Mix vertical and horizontal stacks, include bookends or art, and avoid overpacking. Want to display your whole collection? Color-block them! It creates visual harmony and a touch of personality.

📚 Local Pro Hack: Place books you're less likely to use on higher shelves or in secondary rooms. Let your shelf space breathe.

4. Keep Furniture Fuss-Free

Too many throw pillows, rumpled blankets, or oversized slipcovers can make even the cleanest space feel disheveled. Stick to one or two pillows per seat, and fold throws neatly.

🛎️ Saint Louis Tip: If your furniture faces heavy use (kids, pets, entertaining), choose washable slipcovers and schedule a monthly deep-clean from our team at Maid 4 Cleaning. Call us at (314) 626-5885 for a refresh.

5. Hide the Cord Chaos

We love tech—smart TVs, chargers, speakers—but not the jungle of cords that comes with them. Use cord clips, boxes, or even wrap cords in jute rope for a rustic DIY look.

🔌 Skip taping cords to walls or baseboards—it’s not only unsightly but can be unsafe.

6. Curate, Don’t Clutter, the Fridge

Your kitchen may sparkle, but if your fridge is plastered in magnets and expired coupons, that’s what people notice. Limit what’s on display and rotate kids' art weekly. Bonus: decluttering the fridge’s interior helps reduce food waste!

🧲 Pro Organizer Tip: Store coupons and menus in a magnetic file or dedicated binder.

7. Let Natural Light and Air Flow In

Even the best décor can’t outshine the impact of natural light and fresh air. Keep windows clean, blinds dusted, and use light or sheer curtains to maximize brightness. Light visually expands a room and makes it feel cleaner and calmer.

🌿 Bonus: Indoor plants are a chic way to decorate while naturally improving air quality.
