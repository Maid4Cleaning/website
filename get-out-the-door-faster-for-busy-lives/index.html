Get Out the Door Faster for Busy Lives

Life in Saint Louis can be a whirlwind. Between work, school runs, and the occasional Blues game, it’s no wonder our homes get a little cluttered. Still, nothing throws off your morning mojo like walking out the door and seeing a mess in your wake.

That’s where quick cleaning habits come in handy. You don’t need to do a full deep clean every day. A few smart, strategic habits can help you keep your space company-ready, stress-free, and looking fresh. And if things get too far gone? That’s why we’re here.

Here are our best fast cleaning tips to help you get out the door faster—and come home to peace and calm.

1. Know What Takes Seconds to Clean

Some tasks feel huge but take less time than scrolling your feed. Here are a few cleaning wins that take less than a minute:

  • Make your bed – It instantly makes the room look put-together.

  • Wipe down the bathroom counter while your coffee brews.

  • Clear dishes from the sink and start the dishwasher before heading out.

  • Throw laundry in the washer in the morning; switch it to the dryer when you get home.

  • Grab trash on your way out – If you’re headed to your car, take it to the bin.

  • Do a 5-minute pick-up each evening. Use a laundry basket to gather items that don’t belong and put them in the right spot.

Doing small tasks when they pop up helps prevent big messes later. It’s all about momentum.

2. Channel Your Inner “Neat Freak” (Without the Stress)

Even if perfection isn’t your vibe, you can learn from people who always seem to have clean homes:

  • Put things away right after use. This alone makes a massive difference.

  • Teach kids the same habit. A labeled bin system helps—even toddlers get it!

  • Do a 10-minute family cleanup. Set a timer, turn on music, and make it fun.

  • Keep surfaces clear. Clutter attracts more clutter. Clear counters feel cleaner even when they aren’t spotless.

3. Build Better Cleaning Habits Over Time

Want to spend less time cleaning? Create habits that make cleaning easier by default:

  • Keep cleaning supplies where you use them. Glass cleaner in the bathroom, all-purpose spray under the kitchen sink.

  • Use microfiber cloths. They clean better with less effort—and just water in many cases​.

  • Create a “drop zone” at your entryway. A tray for keys, a hook for bags, a basket for shoes—this tames the chaos from the minute you walk in.

4. Create a Realistic Cleaning Schedule

Quick cleans work better when paired with a basic cleaning routine. Here’s a sample weekly schedule you can tweak:

  • Monday – Mirrors and glass

  • Tuesday – Toilets and showers

  • Wednesday – Change sheets and towels

  • Thursday – Dust and wipe surfaces

  • Friday – Vacuum and mop floors

  • Weekend – Rest… or call Maid 4 Cleaning!

Want to get the whole family involved? Use your Wi-Fi password as motivation—it works wonders.
