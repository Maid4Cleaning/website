How Often Should You Wash Your Sheets? The Truth Might Surprise You

Let’s be honest, changing the sheets isn’t everyone’s favorite chore. But if your idea of “cleaning” the bed is flipping the pillow over to the cool side, it might be time for a quick refresh on why regular sheet washing matters.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we’ve helped countless Saint Louis homes stay fresh and comfortable. So, let’s break down why washing your sheets isn’t just a “nice to do” it’s a must-do for your health and peace of mind.

How Often Should You Really Wash Your Sheets?

Ideally, you should wash your sheets once a week. We get it that’s more often than most people manage. In fact, nearly half of people only wash their sheets once or twice a month. But with everything your bed collects, weekly washing is worth the effort.

Why You Need to Wash Bed Sheets Weekly

While you're snoozing, your body is busy shedding skin cells, hair, and sweat. That cozy little nest of yours also collects:

  • Dust mites (they love skin flakes)

  • Allergens like pollen and pet dander

  • Oils and body fluids

  • Bacteria and fungi (especially if you have athlete’s foot, acne, or eczema)

  • Pet germs (Mittens may be adorable, but she's not sterile)

The longer you go between washes, the more you’re sleeping in a buildup of allergens, grime, and microbes that can impact your skin, sinuses, and sleep quality.

Pro Tips from Cleaning Experts

Want to make weekly washing doable? Try these smart strategies from the pros:

  • Keep two or more sheet sets. When one comes off, another goes on, no excuses.

  • Wash in hot water (130–140°F) to kill bacteria and dust mites (check the label first).

  • Use a fragrance-free detergent if you're sensitive to allergens.

  • Dry thoroughly to prevent mildew.

  • Add ½ cup of white vinegar during the rinse cycle to soften sheets and neutralize odors.

And skip the whole “baking soda and vinegar” combo, those two cancel each other out when mixed. Instead, use each separately where appropriate.

What If You Don’t Have In-Unit Laundry?

No problem! If you're in an apartment or rely on a laundromat, aim to wash your sheets every 1–2 weeks, and rotate between clean sets. Can’t get to it? Consider using a local drop-off laundry service, or give us a call - we’ll handle it all.
