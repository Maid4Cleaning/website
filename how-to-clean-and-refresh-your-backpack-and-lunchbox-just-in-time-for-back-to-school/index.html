How to Clean and Refresh Your Backpack and Lunchbox – Just in Time for Back to School

Summer always seems to fly by, and before you know it, it's time to get the kids—and maybe yourself—ready for school or work again. Whether you're reusing last year’s backpack or lunchbox or just bought new ones, giving them a good clean is a smart way to start the season fresh.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know the importance of keeping your daily gear as clean and germ-free as your home. Here’s how to clean backpacks and insulated lunch bags the right way to keep them looking (and smelling) like new.

Start With a Check: Can You Machine Wash It?

Before you do anything, check the care label. Some backpacks and lunchboxes have foam insulation, leather accents, or special coatings that don’t do well in a washing machine. If there’s no label, treat it like a delicate item and hand wash to be safe.

Cleaning a Backpack in the Washing Machine

If your backpack is machine-washable, here’s how to clean it effectively without causing damage:

What You’ll Need:

  • Mild liquid laundry detergent (no bleach)

  • A mesh laundry bag or an old pillowcase

  • Optional: stain remover and a soft toothbrush

Steps:

  1. Empty It Out: Check every pocket and give it a good shake. Use a handheld vacuum or a lint roller to pick up crumbs or dust.

  2. Pre-Treat Stains: Use a bit of laundry detergent or stain remover and gently scrub with a toothbrush.

  3. Bag It: Turn the backpack inside out and place it in a pillowcase or mesh bag to protect straps and zippers.

  4. Wash on Gentle: Use cold water and a gentle cycle. Avoid fabric softener.

  5. Hang to Dry: Air dry upside down, preferably outdoors but out of direct sunlight to prevent fading.

📍 Pro Tip for St. Louis humidity: Make sure it's completely dry before putting anything back inside. Mold can sneak in fast!

Hand Washing a Backpack

If your backpack has leather, suede, or metal accents—or just seems too delicate for the machine—hand washing is your best bet.

What You’ll Need:

  • A mild detergent or castile soap

  • Warm water

  • A microfiber cloth or soft brush

  • A bucket or clean sink

Steps:

  1. Remove all contents and turn the backpack inside out.

  2. Mix warm water with a small amount of detergent.

  3. Dip the cloth or brush in the solution and gently scrub the bag, avoiding sensitive trim or leather.

  4. Rinse using a clean damp cloth.

  5. Dry with a towel and hang it upside down to fully air dry.

How to Clean a Lunchbox

Soft, insulated lunchboxes need special care to preserve the lining.

What You’ll Need:

  • Dish soap

  • Warm water

  • A soft sponge or cloth

  • Optional: lemon juice or white vinegar for odor control

Steps:

  1. Turn the lunchbox inside out to expose the lining.

  2. Mix warm water and dish soap in a bowl.

  3. Use a sponge to clean the interior, being sure not to soak it.

  4. Wipe the exterior gently.

  5. Allow the lunchbox to fully air dry before using.

Dealing With Stains and Odors in Lunchboxes

Forgotten food spills can leave both stains and lingering smells. Here’s a gentle way to tackle both:

Natural Deodorizing Paste:

  • Mix baking soda and water to form a paste.

  • Apply to stained areas and let sit for 10–15 minutes.

  • Wipe clean with a damp cloth.

Extra Tip: Let the lunchbox dry in the sun—sunlight naturally helps kill odor-causing bacteria and freshens up that inside lining.

Keeping Bags Fresh All Year Long

  • Use a weekly wipe-down routine with disinfecting wipes.

  • Dry bags completely after every cleaning or spill.

  • Store open and unzipped to prevent trapped moisture and odors.

  • Toss a dryer sheet or small satchel of baking soda inside to absorb odors between uses.
