How to Clean Granite Floors Like a Pro: A Step-by-Step Guide

Granite floors add elegance and durability to any home, but keeping them in pristine condition requires the right cleaning approach. Unlike other types of flooring, granite is a porous natural stone that can be easily damaged by harsh chemicals and improper cleaning methods. If you're looking for a foolproof way to keep your granite floors spotless and polished, follow these expert-approved cleaning tips.

Why Proper Granite Floor Cleaning Matters

Over time, dirt, dust, and spills can wear down the protective seal on your granite floors, causing them to lose their luster. Regular maintenance not only extends the life of your flooring but also enhances the beauty of your home. Whether you’re dealing with everyday grime or tough stains, using the right cleaning techniques will prevent etching, dullness, and damage.

What You’ll Need:

  • Bucket
  • Warm water
  • pH-neutral stone cleaner or mild detergent
  • Soft mop or microfiber cloth
  • Soft towel for drying

Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Granite Floors

1. Sweep or Dry Mop Regularly

Before applying any liquid cleaner, remove dust, dirt, and debris with a soft broom or microfiber mop. This prevents tiny particles from scratching the granite surface over time.

2. Wipe Up Spills Immediately

Granite is porous and can absorb liquids quickly, leading to stains. Clean up spills as soon as they happen using a damp cloth. Avoid acidic substances like vinegar, citrus, or wine, as they can cause etching.

3. Use a pH-Neutral Cleaner

Harsh chemicals like bleach, ammonia, and vinegar can dull and damage the surface of granite. Instead, use a mild detergent or a specialized stone cleaner diluted in warm water.

4. Mop with a Soft Cloth or Mop

  • Dip a soft mop into the cleaning solution and wring out excess water to avoid oversaturating the floor.
  • Mop the floor gently in small sections, ensuring that you’re cleaning thoroughly without leaving puddles behind.

5. Dry the Floor to Prevent Streaks

Using a clean, dry microfiber towel or mop, dry the floor to prevent water spots and streaking.

6. Buff for Extra Shine (Optional)

For an added shine, buff the dry granite floor with a soft, dry cloth or a polishing pad designed for stone surfaces.

Granite Floor Cleaning Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Skipping regular maintenance – Dirt buildup can scratch and dull the surface over time.
❌ Using acidic or harsh cleaners – They can weaken the seal and cause permanent damage.
❌ Allowing water to sit on the floor – Standing water can seep into the pores and cause discoloration.
❌ Using rough scrubbing tools – Steel wool and abrasive pads can scratch and ruin the polished finish.

Pro Tip for St. Louis Homes:

Due to the humid climate in St. Louis, it’s crucial to keep granite floors dry after mopping. Excess moisture can lead to mold and mildew growth in the grout lines, which can be difficult to clean.
