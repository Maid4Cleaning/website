How to Clean Lampshades: The Ultimate Guide

If you regularly clean your lampshades, give yourself a pat on the back! For many people, lampshades are one of those household items that get overlooked during routine cleaning. However, they tend to collect dust, allergens, and even discolor over time. The good news? Cleaning lampshades isn’t as tricky as you might think! Here’s how to tackle lampshades of all shapes, sizes, and materials—and how to keep them looking fresh for longer.

Cleaning Fabric Lampshades

Fabric lampshades can be a bit more challenging to clean, but the results are well worth it. Follow these steps for a thorough clean:

  1. Check the Label – Before starting, check the manufacturer’s instructions for any special cleaning recommendations.

  2. Dust First – Use a dry microfiber cloth or a vacuum with an upholstery attachment to remove surface dust.

  3. Prepare a Cleaning Solution – Fill a bathtub with warm water and add a few drops of mild liquid detergent.

  4. Submerge and Soak – Gently lower the lampshade into the water, letting it soak for about 10 minutes.

  5. Hand-Wash with Care – Use a clean microfiber cloth to wipe the shade from top to bottom.

  6. Rinse Thoroughly – Drain the tub, refill with clean water, and swish the shade to remove soap residue.

  7. Dry Properly – Shake off excess water and place the lampshade on a towel to air dry. You can also set it outside on a dry day or use a hairdryer on low heat.

  8. Reattach – Once completely dry, place the lampshade back on the lamp.

Cleaning Paper, Parchment, or Glue-Adhesive Lampshades

Paper and parchment shades require extra caution as they can be easily damaged by moisture.

  1. Wear Gloves – The oils on your hands can stain delicate materials.

  2. Dust Gently – Use a dry microfiber cloth or a vacuum attachment to remove dust.

  3. Bread Trick for Parchment Shades – Believe it or not, a piece of white bread can help lift dirt and stains from parchment shades. Gently rub the bread over the surface to absorb dirt.

Cleaning Plastic Lampshades

Plastic shades are the easiest to clean due to their water-resistant nature.

  1. Remove Dust – Wipe with a dry microfiber cloth or vacuum attachment.

  2. Use Soapy Water – Mix warm water with a few drops of mild dish soap.

  3. Wipe Clean – Dampen a microfiber cloth with the solution and wipe the shade.

  4. Dry Completely – Allow it to dry fully before reattaching to the lamp.

Routine Maintenance for Lampshades

Once you’ve deep cleaned your lampshades, it’s important to maintain them to prevent future buildup. Here’s a simple routine:

  • Weekly Dusting – Use a microfiber cloth or a lint roller to remove dust.

  • Vacuum Regularly – A vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment is great for pleated or fabric shades.

  • Avoid Direct Sunlight – Prolonged exposure can cause fading and yellowing.

Final Thoughts

Cleaning your lampshades is a small task that makes a big impact on your home’s overall cleanliness. With these easy methods, your lampshades will stay dust-free, bright, and fresh!
