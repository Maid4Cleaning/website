How to Clean Smelly Dishwashing Gloves (and Keep Them That Way)

Dishwashing gloves are a kitchen essential. They protect your hands from harsh cleaners, hot water, and all the mess that comes with keeping your Saint Louis home sparkling. But let’s be honest—they can get pretty stinky. If you’ve ever peeled off a glove and been hit with that rubbery funk, you’re not alone.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we believe your tools should work for you, not against you. Here’s how to properly clean and care for your gloves, say goodbye to that rubber smell, and keep your kitchen experience fresh and pleasant.

Step-by-Step: How to Clean Rubber Gloves

Cleaning your gloves isn't just about comfort—it’s about hygiene. The inside of your gloves is a warm, moist space that bacteria love. Luckily, you can clean them in just a few minutes.

You’ll need:

  • Mild dish soap

  • Warm water

  • A towel or drying rack

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the outside after use to remove any residue.

  2. Wash the outer surface with dish soap. You can gently scrub with a soft sponge if needed.

  3. Rinse thoroughly to remove any soap.

  4. Air dry completely, ideally hanging them upside down.

  5. Once dry, turn them inside out and repeat steps 1 through 4 on the interior.

⚠️ Pro tip: If your gloves are lined with fabric or foam, check the label or packaging before washing. Some styles need special care.

How to Get Rid of That Lingering Rubber Smell

That latex or rubbery odor is a common complaint. Fortunately, we have a few tried-and-true tricks that work wonders:

1. Baking Soda Boost

Sprinkle a little baking soda inside your gloves once they’re dry. This helps absorb odors and moisture. For an extra fresh touch, mix in a drop or two of lavender or citrus essential oil before you sprinkle.

2. Use Hand Lotion Before Wearing

Apply a scented, skin-safe lotion before slipping your gloves on. It not only adds a pleasant smell but also protects your skin from drying out.

3. Scented Soap Soak

Turn your gloves inside out and wash them using a fragrant antibacterial hand soap. Let them soak for a couple of minutes, then rinse and let them dry fully.

Glove Shopping Tips: Prevent the Stink Before It Starts

Not all gloves are created equal. If smelly gloves are a regular issue, try these options:

  • Latex-free gloves (made with nitrile or vinyl) often have less odor

  • Pre-scented gloves with vanilla or lavender options

  • Cotton- or foam-lined gloves that wick away sweat and feel better against your skin
