How to Clean Up Vomit Like a Pro

Be Swept Away with Maid 4 Cleaning

We've all had those moments — your child wakes up sick in the middle of the night, your dog chooses the new rug, or someone didn’t quite make it to the bathroom. No one wants to clean up vomit, but when life happens, Maid 4 Cleaning is here to help you tackle it efficiently and effectively, without all the panic.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Up Vomit

1. Get Your Supplies Ready
You’ll need:

  • Gloves (a must)

  • Paper towels or disposable cloths

  • Trash bags

  • Baking soda or an enzymatic cleaner

  • Disinfectant spray

  • Mild dish soap

  • Bucket of warm water

  • Vacuum (for carpets)

  • Fan or open window for ventilation

2. Scoop It Up
Wearing gloves, gently scoop up as much of the mess as possible using paper towels or a plastic dustpan. Place the waste directly into a sealed trash bag.

3. Blot, Don’t Scrub
Use paper towels or an absorbent cloth to blot — not rub — the area. Rubbing forces the vomit deeper into fabrics and fibers. Add warm water with a small amount of dish soap to help loosen residue if needed.

4. Disinfect Thoroughly
Spray the area with a hospital-grade disinfectant or one approved to kill viruses and bacteria. Let it sit for the dwell time listed on the label (typically 5–10 minutes). This is key to neutralizing germs, especially if you’re dealing with stomach flu or a virus.

5. Deodorize with Baking Soda or Enzymes
Once dry, sprinkle baking soda liberally over the spot and let it sit for at least 30 minutes. For pet vomit or lingering smells, an enzymatic cleaner (like Nature’s Miracle) will break down proteins and odors more effectively.

6. Vacuum and Final Wipe Down
Vacuum up the baking soda thoroughly. For hard floors, use a clean, damp mop with mild detergent to finish. For carpet, follow up with a carpet-safe cleaner if staining remains.

7. Ventilate the Room
Open a window or use a fan to air out any lingering smells. Fresh air speeds up drying and clears out odor particles.

What If You Can’t Clean It Right Away?

Sometimes life gets in the way. Here’s how to buy yourself time:

  • Cover the area with baking soda to absorb moisture and odor.

  • Lay a towel or rag over it to contain the mess.

  • Once ready, follow the regular steps: scoop, blot, disinfect, deodorize, vacuum.

Tip: Avoid scrubbing dried vomit too hard, especially on carpets. This can damage fibers. Instead, rehydrate the stain with a damp cloth before treating it.

Pro Tips from the Experts at Maid 4 Cleaning

  • For carpets or upholstery, always test cleaners on an inconspicuous spot first.

  • Use microfiber cloths — they clean more effectively and can be washed and reused.

  • Steam cleaners are excellent for deep sanitizing fabric surfaces after cleaning.

  • Keep a “yuck kit” in your cleaning closet with gloves, enzyme spray, trash bags, and baking soda. You’ll thank yourself later.
