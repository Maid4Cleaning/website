How to Get Rid of Glitter: A Cleaning Guide for Every Surface

Glitter is fun and festive, adding sparkle to arts and crafts, holiday decorations, and even makeup. But when it spills, it can seem impossible to remove, sticking to every surface and lingering for weeks. If you've found yourself battling an unexpected glitter explosion, don't worry—these tried-and-true cleaning techniques will help you reclaim your space with minimal effort.

Best Ways to Remove Glitter from Different Surfaces

1. Lint Rollers: Your First Line of Defense

Lint rollers are incredibly effective for picking up glitter from fabric, upholstery, and hard surfaces. Simply roll the adhesive over the glittered area, peeling away layers as needed. This works well for:

  • Sofas and chairs
  • Clothing and fabric
  • Table surfaces

Pro Tip: If you don’t have a lint roller, wrap duct tape or masking tape around your hand, sticky side out, and dab the affected areas.

2. Vacuuming: Essential for Carpets & Floors

If glitter has landed on carpets, rugs, or even hard floors, a vacuum cleaner with a hose attachment is your best bet. For optimal results:

  • Use a handheld vacuum or the hose attachment for precise suction.
  • If on hardwood or tile, follow up with a microfiber cloth to remove any stubborn pieces.
  • For plush carpets, try lightly brushing with a rubber squeegee before vacuuming to lift embedded glitter.

3. Microfiber Cloths for Finishing Touches

A slightly damp microfiber cloth can grab any stray pieces of glitter still clinging to surfaces. Use a light patting motion on furniture, countertops, and even skin.

Caution: Check fabric cleaning instructions before using a damp cloth on upholstered furniture.

4. Play Dough or Sticky Tack for Tiny Spots

For small spaces or hard-to-reach areas, press play dough, sticky tack, or a wad of tape onto the glitter. This technique works especially well on keyboards, cracks in furniture, and tight corners.

5. Swiffer Dry or Wet Cloths for Hard Surfaces

For hardwood, tile, or linoleum floors, a Swiffer dry cloth will attract and trap glitter efficiently. For a more thorough clean, follow up with a damp Swiffer or mop to pick up any remaining specks.

Preventing Future Glitter Catastrophes

Even though cleaning glitter can be a challenge, you can prevent major messes with a few proactive strategies:

  • Designate a Glitter Zone: If you or your kids enjoy glitter crafts, limit usage to a single area, preferably one with easy-to-clean floors.
  • Use Trays or Kraft Paper: Place a large tray or kraft paper under any glitter-related project to contain the spread.
  • Change Clothes After Glitter Use: Glitter sticks to clothing and transfers easily throughout the house. Have kids change clothes immediately after a craft session and wash garments separately.
  • Seal Glitter Projects: If you’re using glitter on artwork or crafts, consider sealing it with a spray adhesive to prevent fallout over time.
