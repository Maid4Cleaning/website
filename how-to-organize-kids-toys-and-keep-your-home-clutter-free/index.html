How to Organize Kids' Toys and Keep Your Home Clutter-Free

Every parent knows the pain of stepping on a rogue building block or tripping over a stuffed animal. Kids' toys have a way of spreading across every corner of your home, making it feel like a never-ending battle to keep things tidy. But don’t worry—organizing kids' toys doesn’t have to be overwhelming. With a few smart strategies, you can reclaim your space while also teaching your little ones valuable organization habits.

Make Storage Fun and Engaging

Kids are more likely to use storage solutions if they’re fun and appealing. Consider using bright and colorful storage bins with cartoon characters or animals to encourage them to put their toys away. Repurpose items like old dresser drawers or suitcases to create unique under-bed storage. Hanging storage options, such as a "hungry monster" plush toy bag, can make tidying up feel like a game rather than a chore.

Dedicate a Toy Zone

Instead of letting toys migrate throughout the house, designate a specific area where all the toys belong. Use cubby shelves with labeled bins so your children know exactly where their favorite stuffed animals, dolls, and action figures should go. Install a pocket bookshelf to display books with covers facing outward, making it easy for little hands to choose their next bedtime story.

Enhance this space by incorporating a creative display of your child’s artwork using twine and clothespins. Not only does this add personality to their play area, but it also makes the space feel special and well-loved.

Smart Bath Toy Storage

Toys in the bathtub can quickly become a cluttered, soggy mess. Opt for storage solutions that allow for easy drying to prevent mildew. Attach adhesive hooks to the shower wall and hang mesh bags—similar to the ones used for storing produce—to hold bath toys. Alternatively, install a tension rod along the shower wall and use hanging plastic bins to store rubber ducks, squirting toys, and floating boats.

Hide Toys in Common Areas

If you keep toys in your living room or family space, opt for storage solutions that blend seamlessly with your décor. Wicker baskets, ottomans with built-in storage, and shelving with fabric bins allow you to tuck toys away when guests arrive. For a clever hidden storage solution, install a tension rod on a bookshelf and hang a stylish curtain to keep toys out of sight when playtime is over.

Rotate Toys for Less Clutter

Over time, kids can accumulate a massive toy collection, but they don’t need access to everything at once. Try a toy rotation system: store half of the toys in a closet or storage box and swap them out every few weeks. This keeps things fresh and exciting while also reducing clutter in your everyday space.

Teach Kids Simple Clean-Up Habits

Even young children can learn to clean up after themselves when it’s part of their routine. Set a timer and make it a race to put away as many toys as possible before time runs out. Use picture labels on bins for younger children who can’t yet read, and encourage them to put items in the right place.
