How to Remove Coffee Stains from Mugs

We all have that one favorite coffee mug — whether it’s stamped with a funny quote, your alma mater’s crest, or a charming chipped handle that’s seen better days. But if your mug has coffee stains clinging to the inside like a permanent resident, don’t worry. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know a thing or two about fighting tough stains with easy solutions.

Here's how to give your mug a fresh start using simple ingredients you likely already have at home or at the office.

1. Lemon and Salt: Nature’s Dynamic Duo

Lemon juice isn’t just great in tea — it’s a powerful natural cleanser. The citric acid breaks down stains, while salt provides gentle abrasion.

What you’ll need:

  • 1 fresh lemon (quartered)

  • A pinch of salt

  • Microfiber cloth

  • Warm water

How to do it:

  1. Squeeze lemon juice directly into your mug, focusing on stained areas.

  2. Add a pinch of salt to boost scrubbing power.

  3. Let it sit for 10–15 minutes.

  4. Use a damp microfiber cloth to scrub the inside of the mug.

  5. Rinse with warm water and repeat if needed.

2. Baking Soda Paste: Gentle and Effective

Baking soda is a mild abrasive that’s safe for ceramics and great at lifting stains.

You’ll need:

  • 1–2 teaspoons baking soda

  • A few drops of water

  • Microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Mix baking soda with a little water to create a paste.

  2. Spread the paste over stained areas of your mug.

  3. Scrub gently using a dry microfiber cloth.

  4. Rinse thoroughly.

  5. Repeat if needed for deep-set stains.

Tip: Avoid using metal scrubbing pads, which can damage your mug’s glaze.

3. Salt Scrub: Perfect for the Office Mug

Forgot to bring your lemon or baking soda to work? Salt alone can help in a pinch.

Grab:

  • 1 tablespoon of salt

  • Water

  • A microfiber cloth

Instructions:

  1. Wet the inside of the mug.

  2. Sprinkle salt generously over the stains.

  3. Scrub with your cloth until the stains lift.

  4. Rinse clean and admire your shiny mug.

4. Vinegar Soak (Without the Mix!)

White vinegar works wonders on tannin stains like those from coffee and tea.

You’ll need:

  • 1 cup white vinegar

  • Microwave-safe bowl

  • Oven mitts

Steps:

  1. Heat the vinegar in a bowl for 90 seconds to 2 minutes.

  2. Carefully pour the hot vinegar into your mug.

  3. Let it sit for 3 to 4 hours, or overnight for heavy stains.

  4. Rinse and wash as usual.

Bonus Tip: Use a Magic Eraser

For stubborn discoloration, a clean dampened Magic Eraser can lift stains without chemicals. Just test on a small area first to avoid damage.
