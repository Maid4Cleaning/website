How to Remove Rust Stains from Your Metal Kitchen Items

If you love spending time in the kitchen, you know how frustrating it can be to see rust spots appear on your favorite cooking tools. Rust not only affects the appearance of your cookware, but it can also compromise its functionality and longevity. The good news? You don’t have to replace your rusted pots, pans, and knives just yet. With a few simple DIY methods, you can restore your metal kitchen items and keep them looking brand new!

How to Remove Rust from Knives

Your go-to kitchen knife is irreplaceable, so any rust spots need to be taken care of immediately.

What You’ll Need:

  • Distilled white vinegar

  • A glass or container large enough to hold your knife

  • A scrubbing sponge

  • A dry microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Fill the glass with vinegar.

  2. Submerge the rusted part of the knife in the vinegar. If it’s a large knife with a heavy handle, secure the glass to prevent tipping.

  3. Let the knife soak for about five minutes.

  4. Remove the knife and lightly scrub rusted areas with the scouring side of your sponge.

  5. Rinse under cold water.

  6. Dry thoroughly with a microfiber cloth to prevent future rusting.

How to Remove Rust from Metal Baking Pans

Rust on baking pans can affect the taste and quality of your baked goods, so keeping them rust-free is essential.

What You’ll Need:

  • A raw potato

  • Dishwashing liquid or baking soda

  • A dry microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Cut the potato in half.

  2. Dip the cut end in dishwashing liquid or baking soda.

  3. Scrub the rusted area with the potato. If it starts to slip too much, cut off a fresh section and continue.

  4. Repeat until all rust is gone.

  5. Rinse thoroughly under running water.

  6. Dry completely with a microfiber cloth to avoid future rust formation.

How to Remove Rust from Cast Iron Cookware

Rust on your cast-iron skillet can be alarming, but don’t worry—it can be restored!

What You’ll Need:

  • A potato

  • Kosher salt

  • A kitchen towel

  • Newspaper, baking parchment, or something similar to protect your workspace

Steps:

  1. Lay down newspaper or parchment paper to contain the mess.

  2. Cut the potato in half.

  3. Generously coat the skillet’s surface with kosher salt.

  4. Scrub the rusted areas using the potato, applying firm pressure to remove rust.

  5. If the potato end gets too slick, cut off a fresh portion and continue.

  6. If the salt gets dirty, rinse the pan and reapply fresh salt.

  7. Once rust is removed, rinse the skillet with water and dry with a kitchen towel.

  8. Re-season the skillet to maintain its protective layer and prevent future rusting.

Preventing Rust on Kitchen Metalware

To keep your metal kitchen tools rust-free, follow these tips:

  • Dry immediately: After washing, always dry metal items thoroughly.

  • Apply oil: Lightly coat cast iron and carbon steel knives with vegetable oil after cleaning.

  • Store properly: Keep metal utensils in a dry place to avoid excess moisture exposure.

  • Use baking soda: Occasionally rub baking soda on metal surfaces to prevent oxidation.
