How to Vacuum Properly: Expert Tips for a Cleaner Home

Vacuuming is an essential part of maintaining a clean home, especially if you have carpets or rugs. While many people assume that vacuuming is as simple as plugging in the machine and pushing it around, there's actually a right way to do it for maximum effectiveness.

Check out these expert vacuuming tips to see if you’re getting the most out of your cleaning routine.

1. Establish a Vacuuming Routine

Waiting until your floors visibly look dirty is a common mistake. By the time you notice dirt buildup, your carpets are already embedded with dust and debris that require deep cleaning. Instead, incorporate vacuuming into your regular cleaning schedule. High-traffic areas should be vacuumed at least two to three times per week, while less frequently used spaces can be done weekly.

2. Slow and Steady Wins the Race

One of the biggest mistakes people make is rushing through vacuuming. Moving too quickly over the surface doesn’t allow the vacuum to properly lift dirt and dust from carpet fibers. For best results, push the vacuum slowly forward and then pull it back over the same area. This back-and-forth motion allows the vacuum’s vibrations to loosen debris, making it easier to remove.

3. Focus on High-Traffic Areas

Hallways, entryways, mudrooms, and living room floors accumulate more dirt than other areas of the home. These high-traffic zones require more frequent vacuuming—sometimes as often as every other day. Pay extra attention to these areas by making multiple passes with the vacuum to lift embedded dirt.

4. Keep Your Vacuum Clean

A clogged vacuum can’t perform efficiently. Empty the canister or replace the vacuum bag regularly—ideally after each use. Additionally, clean the filters and brush rollers to prevent dirt buildup. A well-maintained vacuum improves suction power, ensuring a deeper clean every time.

5. Choose the Right Vacuum and Attachments

Different flooring types require different vacuuming techniques. If you have plush carpets, a vacuum with a beater bar will help loosen debris from fibers. However, for delicate rugs with looped fibers or natural materials like wool or silk, avoid beater bars as they can cause damage. Use the right attachments for specific tasks: a crevice tool for corners, an upholstery brush for furniture, and a dusting brush for baseboards and blinds.
