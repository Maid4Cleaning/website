Kitchen Garbage Can Cleaning: The Overlooked Task That Makes a Big Difference

When you're prepping for the holidays, spring cleaning, or just getting your home guest-ready in Saint Louis, the last thing on your list might be your kitchen garbage can. But trust us—this unsung hero of your kitchen deserves some love. After all, it's the first place smells start and the last place you want to ignore.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know that a clean home starts with the details. So here’s how—and why—to clean your kitchen trash can, plus pro tips for keeping it fresh between cleanings.

How Often Should You Clean Your Kitchen Trash Can?

For most households, once a month is the minimum. But if you’re a busy Saint Louis family who cooks often, or you notice lingering odors, consider cleaning it every two weeks.

If you compost, recycle religiously, or eat out frequently, your can might stay fresher longer. Use your nose as your guide—when it starts to smell funky, it's time for a clean.

Step-by-Step: How to Deep Clean Your Kitchen Trash Can

This is easier than you think—and you don’t need fancy products or harsh chemicals.

  1. Empty the can completely—no bag, no crumbs.
  2. Rinse the inside with warm water, ideally outside or in a bathtub.
  3. Clean the exterior first with warm soapy water and a microfiber cloth. This avoids spreading inside grime outward.
  4. Scrub the interior thoroughly. Use a soft brush and a mix of dish soap and hot water. Add a splash of white vinegar for odor neutralization, but skip baking soda if you're using vinegar—it neutralizes the effect!
  5. Rinse and dry completely. Mold and bacteria love moisture, so leave the lid open until it’s bone-dry.
  6. Line it with a fresh trash bag. Done and dusted.

Pro Tips to Keep Your Trash Can Smelling Fresh

Want to stretch the time between deep cleans? Here are some professional-grade hacks:

  • Use liners that fit snugly. Loose liners fall in and create a mess fast.
  • Never toss liquids directly into the trash. Seal them in a bag first.
  • Wipe up any spills or smears immediately. Use a disinfecting wipe or soapy cloth.
  • Avoid the vinegar + baking soda myth. As Dario Bressanini explains in The Science of Cleaning, they cancel each other out. Instead, use baking soda alone in the bottom to absorb odors only if you clean the bin frequently. Otherwise, it can clump and trap moisture.
  • Use deionized water with microfiber cloths for a chemical-free, effective clean (Natural Cleaning Secrets, Reader’s Digest).
  • Buff stainless steel bins with a dry microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and restore shine.

Bonus: Maid 4 Cleaning Tip for Odor Control

Add a cotton ball soaked in essential oil or a citrus peel to the bottom of the bin (under the liner). This keeps it smelling pleasant naturally between cleanings.

Why Trash Can Cleaning Matters in Saint Louis Homes

Especially during holidays and high-traffic times, trash bins become hot spots for bacteria and odors. In humid climates like Saint Louis, moisture and food waste accelerate odor and mold. A clean bin helps prevent pests and keeps your kitchen smelling fresh.
