7 Surprising Ways to Clean with Lemon and Refresh Your Home

Spring is here in Saint Louis — the cardigans are packed away, and your home is (hopefully) post-spring-clean bliss. Now, you might be wondering how to keep that fresh feeling going. One natural solution that smells as good as it works? Lemons.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we love using lemons for their natural acidity, fresh scent, and surprisingly powerful cleaning capabilities. From your microwave to your bathroom fixtures, lemons can do more than just flavor your water. Here are seven of our favorite ways to clean with lemon.

1. Steam-Clean Your Microwave

Microwave messes don’t stand a chance against lemon steam.

What You’ll Need:

  • 1 lemon

  • Microwave-safe bowl

  • 1/2 cup water

How-To: Cut your lemon in half, squeeze the juice into the bowl, drop in the halves, and add the water. Microwave on high for 3 minutes, then let it sit for 2 more. The steam will loosen grime, making it easy to wipe away with a damp cloth.

2. Shine and Deodorize Your Sink

If your sink smells a little funky or looks a little dull, lemon and salt can help scrub it back to beautiful.

You’ll Need:

  • 2 lemons

  • Coarse salt

How-To: Halve the lemons, sprinkle salt around your sink, and use the lemons as natural scrubbers. Rinse with warm water for a clean and citrusy finish.

3. Freshen the Fridge (Without Baking Soda)

Skip the old open-box-of-baking-soda trick. Lemons are a fresher fix.

How-To: Squeeze a bit of lemon juice into a small bowl, or place lemon slices cut-side up in the fridge. Replace weekly for a light, clean scent that neutralizes odors.

4. De-Stain Your Cutting Board

Wooden cutting boards absorb smells and stains easily. Lemon and salt make a great natural cleanser.

You’ll Need:

  • 1/2 lemon

  • Kosher or sea salt

  • Microfiber cloth or scraper

How-To: Sprinkle salt over the board, scrub with lemon while squeezing gently, and let it sit for 5 minutes. Scrape off the salt and rinse. Pat dry and store.

5. Revive a Cloudy Glass Coffee Pot

Heat and coffee can stain your carafe, but lemon to the rescue.

You’ll Need:

  • 1 lemon, quartered

  • 4 tbsp kosher salt

  • 1.5 cups crushed ice

  • 1 cup cool water

How-To: Make sure your coffee pot is cool. Add the ingredients, swirl gently for a few minutes, then rinse well. Avoid drastic temperature changes to prevent cracking.

6. Streak-Free Glass and Mirrors

Lemon juice is a gentle yet effective glass cleaner.

You’ll Need:

  • Juice of 2 lemons (about 3 tbsp)

  • 1 cup water

  • Spray bottle

  • Microfiber cloth

How-To: Combine and shake. Spray on windows or mirrors, then wipe clean with a dry cloth for a streak-free shine and citrus scent.

7. Make Chrome Shine in the Bathroom

Bring life back to dull fixtures.

How-To: Rub a lemon half over faucets and shower heads. Buff dry with a microfiber cloth. The natural acid removes mineral buildup and makes metal gleam.

Bonus: Brighten Your Grout

Grout looking grimy? Try this quick lemon hack.

You’ll Need:

  • 2 lemons

  • Cream of tartar

  • Old toothbrush

How-To: Make a paste with lemon juice and cream of tartar, apply with a toothbrush, scrub gently, and rinse clean.
