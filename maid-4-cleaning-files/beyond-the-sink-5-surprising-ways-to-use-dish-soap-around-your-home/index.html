Beyond the Sink: 5 Surprising Ways to Use Dish Soap Around Your Home

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we’re big fans of smart, simple cleaning solutions—especially when they save you time and money. Dish soap may be your go-to for cleaning greasy plates and silverware, but this humble hero does so much more beyond the sink.

Here are five powerful ways you can use dish soap to keep your Saint Louis home sparkling clean. Give them a try, and if you ever need a hand, call us at (314) 626-5885. Be Swept Away.

1. Tackle Tough Laundry Stains

Dish soap is excellent at cutting grease, making it ideal for pre-treating stubborn stains on clothing. For organic stains like food, sweat, or dirt, try this quick pre-treatment:

What you need:

  • 1 part dish soap

  • 3 parts hydrogen peroxide

Mix the ingredients and apply directly to the stain. Let it sit for 10 to 20 minutes, checking to ensure it’s lifting the stain and not altering fabric color. Then wash as usual. This method works great for food spills, makeup smudges, and even grass stains.

Pro Tip: Always spot-test a small area first, especially on dark or delicate fabrics.

2. Shine Kitchen Counters and Cabinets

Dish soap is one of the safest and most versatile surface cleaners. It’s gentle on wood, laminate, and even stone countertops like granite and quartz.

How to clean:

  • Mix a few drops of dish soap with warm water in a bucket or bowl.

  • Dampen a microfiber cloth and wipe down your surfaces in smooth, straight motions.

  • Rinse with clean water and buff dry with a dry microfiber cloth for a streak-free finish.

Local Bonus: Saint Louis humidity can make kitchen grime stickier. Weekly wipe-downs prevent buildup and extend the life of your surfaces.

3. Banish Soap Scum from the Bathroom

Soap scum builds up fast in showers and tubs. Skip the harsh chemicals and try this natural cleaner made with ingredients already in your home.

What you need:

  • 2 tablespoons dish soap

  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

  • 1 cup distilled white vinegar

Heat the vinegar and cornstarch mixture in the microwave until it thickens (1–2 minutes). Pour it into a spray bottle, add the dish soap, and shake well. Spray it on your tub and shower walls, let it sit for 10 minutes, then rinse clean. Wipe with a dry cloth for a brilliant shine.

Note: Avoid vinegar on stone surfaces like marble or travertine. For those, stick with dish soap and water only.

4. Refresh Your Appliances Inside and Out

Dish soap is a secret weapon for cleaning the inside and outside of many appliances. It's ideal for removing grease and buildup from:

  • Refrigerator handles and shelves

  • Microwave doors and interiors

  • Coffee makers and toaster exteriors

  • Stovetop knobs and hoods

Quick clean: Mix a drop of dish soap with warm water, wipe with a microfiber cloth, then rinse and dry.

Maid 4 Cleaning Pro Tip: Keep a spray bottle of dish soap and water under your sink for quick touch-ups.

5. Clean Floors Without Residue

If your floors need a light clean and you don’t want streaks, a small amount of dish soap can safely cut through grime on tile, vinyl, and sealed hardwood.

How to clean:

  • Fill a bucket with warm water and add just a few drops of dish soap.

  • Mop using a microfiber mop. Wring it well to avoid excess water.

  • Rinse with clean water and dry if needed.

Avoid: Too much soap will leave floors slippery or sticky. A little goes a long way!
