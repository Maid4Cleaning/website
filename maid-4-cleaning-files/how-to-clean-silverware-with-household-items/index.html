How to Clean Silverware with Household Items

Are you preparing for a special occasion and want to bring out your best silverware, only to find it tarnished and dull? You’re not alone! Silver naturally tarnishes over time, especially when exposed to air and moisture. But don’t worry—you can restore its shine using common household items, without the need for expensive polishes. Here’s how!

Why Does Silver Tarnish?

Silver tarnishes due to a chemical reaction with sulfur-containing compounds in the air. This results in a dark, dull layer forming on the surface of your silverware. Regular cleaning and proper storage can help prevent excessive tarnishing.

Best Methods to Clean Silverware Naturally

1. Clean Silverware with Baking Soda and Vinegar

This tried-and-true method uses the power of a natural chemical reaction to lift tarnish off silver.

You’ll Need:

  • A glass baking dish

  • 1 to 2 cups of boiling water (enough to submerge your silverware)

  • ½ cup of distilled white vinegar

  • 1 tablespoon of baking soda

  • 1 tablespoon of sea salt

  • A microfiber cloth

  • Aluminum foil

Instructions:

  1. Line your baking dish with aluminum foil, shiny side up.

  2. Mix the baking soda and sea salt, then sprinkle over the foil.

  3. Pour the vinegar into the dish—watch as it fizzes!

  4. Add the boiling water, being careful not to splash.

  5. Submerge your silverware in a single layer, ensuring each piece touches the foil.

  6. Let sit for 30 seconds to 3 minutes, depending on the level of tarnish.

  7. Use tongs to remove silverware and dry with a microfiber cloth.

  8. Buff for extra shine!

2. Clean Silverware with Toothpaste

Toothpaste isn’t just for brightening your smile—it works wonders on silver, too!

You’ll Need:

  • Non-gel toothpaste

  • A microfiber cloth

  • A soft-bristled toothbrush

  • Warm water

Instructions:

  1. Apply a small amount of toothpaste to a microfiber cloth.

  2. Rub it onto your silverware using gentle circular motions.

  3. For intricate designs, use a soft toothbrush to get into detailed areas.

  4. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

  5. Dry and polish with a microfiber cloth.

3. Clean Silverware with Baking Soda Paste

A baking soda paste is another effective way to remove stubborn tarnish.

You’ll Need:

  • Baking soda

  • Water

  • A microfiber cloth

  • A soft toothbrush

Instructions:

  1. Mix three parts baking soda with one part water to form a thick paste.

  2. Apply the paste to your silverware with a cloth.

  3. Gently rub in circular motions.

  4. Use a toothbrush for engraved or hard-to-reach areas.

  5. Rinse well and dry immediately with a microfiber cloth.

How to Keep Your Silverware Tarnish-Free

  • Store silver in anti-tarnish cloths or bags.

  • Keep silverware in a dry place, away from excess humidity.

  • Use silverware regularly—frequent use helps prevent tarnish buildup.

  • Add silica gel packs to your storage to absorb moisture.

  • Avoid exposing silver to foods like eggs, onions, and mustard, which accelerate tarnishing.
