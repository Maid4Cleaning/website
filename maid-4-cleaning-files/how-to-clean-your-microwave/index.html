Microwaves are one of the busiest appliances in your kitchen, handling daily tasks from reheating leftovers to cooking quick meals. But keeping your microwave clean? How to Quickly Steam Clean Your Microwave

For everyday maintenance, steam cleaning your microwave is a fast and effortless method.

You'll Need:

  • Microwave-safe bowl

  • 1 cup of water

  • 1 tablespoon of white vinegar or lemon juice

  • Microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Fill your bowl with water and add vinegar or lemon juice for extra cleaning power.

  2. Place the bowl in the microwave and heat on high for 3-5 minutes. Allow it to steam up inside.

  3. Wait a few minutes to let the steam loosen grime safely.

  4. Carefully remove the bowl—it will be hot—and wipe down the microwave interior with a microfiber cloth.

  5. Remove and wash the turntable with warm, soapy water, or pop it in the dishwasher if it's safe to do so.

How to Deep Clean Your Microwave with Baking Soda

When facing stubborn, baked-on stains, baking soda is your best friend.

You'll Need:

  • 4 tablespoons baking soda

  • 1 quart warm water

  • Microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Mix baking soda and water to create a thick paste.

  2. Apply this paste inside your microwave, focusing on heavy stains.

  3. Gently scrub using your microfiber cloth.

  4. Wipe clean with a fresh, damp cloth and repeat if necessary.

  5. Dry thoroughly with a dry microfiber cloth.

Cleaning Your Microwave’s Exterior

The outside matters too! A clean exterior prevents germs and makes your kitchen look great.

You'll Need:

  • Microfiber cloth

  • All-purpose cleaner or vinegar-water solution

Steps:

  1. Lightly spray cleaner onto a cloth (not directly onto the microwave).

  2. Wipe down the microwave’s exterior, focusing on handles, buttons, and frequently touched areas.

  3. Dry and buff with a clean cloth to prevent streaks.

Tips to Keep Your Microwave Cleaner, Longer

  • Cover food while heating to prevent splatters.

  • Clean spills immediately to avoid tougher messes later.

  • Quickly wipe down the microwave weekly.

  • Follow cooking guidelines to prevent food explosions.
