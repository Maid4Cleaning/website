How to Clean Your Wicker Baskets

Wicker baskets add warmth, texture, and functionality to any home. They serve as perfect organizational tools for mail, toiletries, electronics, or cozy blankets. However, their intricate weaves are magnets for dust and grime, making regular cleaning essential to maintain their beauty.

Best Ways to Clean Wicker Baskets

1. Regular Dusting with a Microfiber Cloth

Microfiber cloths are excellent for trapping dust and dirt without leaving lint behind.

What You’ll Need:

  • Dry microfiber cloth
  • Mild liquid dish soap
  • A bucket or large bowl
  • Soft-bristle brush (optional for deep crevices)

Steps:

  1. Empty the basket and shake it out to remove loose dirt.
  2. Use a dry microfiber cloth to wipe down all surfaces.
  3. Mix a few drops of dishwashing liquid with warm water.
  4. Slightly dampen a clean microfiber cloth and gently wipe the wicker. Avoid soaking the basket, as excess moisture can weaken the fibers.
  5. If needed, use a soft-bristle brush to reach tight crevices.
  6. Wipe dry with a clean microfiber cloth and let air dry completely before using.

2. Deep Cleaning with a Vacuum Cleaner

Vacuuming is the easiest way to remove stubborn dust buildup from woven wicker.

What You’ll Need:

  • Vacuum with a brush attachment
  • Microfiber duster

Steps:

  1. Remove all items from the basket.
  2. Use a microfiber duster for an initial wipe-down.
  3. Attach the brush nozzle to your vacuum and gently clean the entire basket, focusing on corners and tight weaves.

3. Removing Mold & Mildew from Wicker Baskets

If your wicker baskets are developing a musty odor or discoloration, mold could be the culprit. Since wicker is a natural material, it’s susceptible to mold in humid environments.

What You’ll Need:

  • Vacuum cleaner
  • Soft-bristle brush or old toothbrush
  • 1:1 mixture of water and white vinegar OR bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water)
  • Gloves
  • Microfiber cloth
  • Hose (optional for outdoor cleaning)

Steps:

  1. Vacuum or dust off any loose mold spores.
  2. Wearing gloves, apply the vinegar or bleach solution with a soft-bristle brush.
  3. Scrub in circular motions, ensuring the solution reaches deep into the weave.
  4. Wipe down with a damp cloth and let the basket air dry completely in the sun.

💡 Pro Tip: Vinegar is a safer alternative to bleach for mold removal, especially for indoor baskets.

4. Restoring and Conditioning Wicker

Over time, wicker can dry out and become brittle. To extend its lifespan, occasional conditioning is recommended.

What You’ll Need:

  • Mineral oil OR linseed oil
  • Soft cloth

Steps:

  1. Apply a small amount of oil to a soft cloth.
  2. Gently rub it over the wicker to restore moisture and prevent cracking.
  3. Let it absorb overnight before using the basket.

5. Preventative Maintenance for Wicker Baskets

  • Avoid placing them in damp areas like basements or bathrooms.
  • Keep them out of direct sunlight to prevent fading and brittleness.
  • Rotate baskets regularly to ensure even wear and tear.
  • Dust weekly to prevent buildup and extend their lifespan.

When to Replace Your Wicker Baskets

If your basket is sagging, breaking, or has a persistent odor that won’t go away despite cleaning, it may be time for a replacement. Refreshing your storage solutions can breathe new life into your home’s décor!
