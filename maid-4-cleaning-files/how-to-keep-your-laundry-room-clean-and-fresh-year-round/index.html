Here’s your expert guide to maintaining a clean, healthy, and efficient laundry room.

Stop Mold in Its Tracks: Washing Machine Maintenance

Despite popular belief, washing machines aren’t self-cleaning. In fact, the moist, enclosed environment is a prime spot for mold and mildew—especially in front-loading washers, where water can collect in the rubber door gasket.

Pro Tips to Prevent Mold:

  • Wipe the gasket and detergent drawer weekly with hot, soapy water.

  • Run a cleaning cycle monthly using a commercial washing machine cleaner—not vinegar and baking soda, which cancel each other out chemically and aren’t effective long-term​.

  • Leave the door open between washes to allow air circulation.

  • Avoid overloading with detergent—excess soap creates residue that feeds mildew.

Get Rid of Musty Dryer Smells

If your dryer smells off or you notice damp clothes after a full cycle, it’s time to clean it.

Here’s what to do:

  • Unplug the dryer before cleaning.

  • Clean the lint trap after every load and wash it monthly with warm, soapy water.

  • Vacuum the interior and exhaust hose twice a year to remove built-up lint (a common fire hazard).

  • Wipe the drum with a microfiber cloth dampened in white vinegar to neutralize odors safely.

If odors persist, have your venting system professionally inspected—especially if there's a burning smell.

Decluttering Your Laundry Room: Space-Saving Hacks

A tidy laundry room isn’t just easier to clean—it’s also safer and more efficient. Clutter can block air flow, attract pests, and hide mold growth.

Quick Decluttering Tips:

  • Use wall-mounted shelves or cabinets to store supplies off the floor.

  • Install hanging rods or fold-away drying racks for space-saving air drying.

  • Use labeled bins or baskets to sort laundry and keep items like stain removers organized.

Need help getting started? Our team at Maid 4 Cleaning can organize your laundry room as part of a deep clean or regular service!

Weekly Laundry Room Cleaning Checklist

Keep your laundry room fresh and functional with this simple weekly routine:

✅ Wipe down washer and dryer (especially knobs and doors)
✅ Clean detergent spills and check for leaks
✅ Dust shelves, appliances, and window sills
✅ Vacuum lint from floor and behind appliances
✅ Empty trash and wipe down trash can
✅ Mop floors with a disinfecting floor-safe solution
✅ Clean utility sink (if present)
✅ Air out the space by opening windows or using a fan
