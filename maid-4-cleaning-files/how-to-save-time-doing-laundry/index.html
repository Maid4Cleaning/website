How to Save Time Doing Laundry

Doing laundry doesn’t have to feel like a never-ending chore. We get it—between busy school weeks, work, sports practices, and everyday life in Saint Louis, it’s easy for the laundry to pile up. Before you know it, those baskets look like bottomless pits.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, our goal is to help you make life easier. While we can’t promise you’ll start loving laundry day, these expert-backed tips can help you get it done faster—and maybe even enjoy the process a little.

1. Set Up a Laundry Room That Works With You

A clean and efficient laundry space sets the tone. Install shelves or cabinets to keep stain removers, dryer sheets, and detergent within reach. Use clear containers so you can see when you’re running low, and hang a drying rack or bar for delicate items. Add a small trash bin for lint and used dryer sheets to reduce clutter.

If you're short on space, a rolling cart stocked with essentials can transform even the tiniest Saint Louis laundry closet into a productive zone.

2. Sort As You Go—Not When You're Tired

Use separate hampers or bins for whites, darks, delicates, towels, and urgent items. Mesh laundry bags for socks can save you from playing the dreaded sock-matching game. This simple change helps you load the machine right away, no extra thinking needed.

Bonus tip: Label hampers so every family member (yes, even the kids!) knows where things go.

3. Stick to a Simple Weekly Laundry Schedule

Break your laundry down by category. Try something like this:

  • Monday & Wednesday: Darks

  • Tuesday & Thursday: Lights

  • Friday: School uniforms or sports gear

  • Saturday or Sunday: Towels and bedding

By doing smaller loads throughout the week, you’ll avoid that overwhelming weekend mountain of laundry. You’ll also keep clothes fresher—no more funky gym clothes sitting around too long.

4. Declutter Your Wardrobe, Shrink Your Laundry

Less clothing means fewer piles to wash. Regularly go through your closets and donate anything you haven’t worn in a year. Look out for items that don’t fit, are out of style, or are damaged beyond repair. Your laundry loads (and your stress) will shrink with your wardrobe.

5. Don’t Leave Clothes in the Basket

We’ve all done it—let clean clothes sit in the basket for days. But folding right away actually saves you time in the long run and helps prevent wrinkles. It also makes it easier to find what you need, when you need it.

Set a timer if you need to and challenge yourself: can you fold and hang everything before your next episode ends?

6. Multitask Like a Pro

Use those wait times during wash and dry cycles to your advantage. You could:

  • Load the dishwasher

  • Wipe down counters

  • Prep lunches or snacks

  • Listen to your favorite Saint Louis podcast or audiobook

You’ll feel extra productive—and your home will thank you.

7. Take Laundry Off Your To-Do List Entirely
