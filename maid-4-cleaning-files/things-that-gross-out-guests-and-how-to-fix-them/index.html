Things That Gross Out Guests (And How to Fix Them)

Hosting guests can be a rewarding experience, but nothing puts a damper on their visit quite like a home that’s secretly (or not so secretly) dirty. Even if you clean regularly, there are certain areas you might overlook—areas your guests definitely notice. To help you prepare for visitors, here’s a checklist of the top things that gross out guests and expert cleaning tips from Maid 4 Cleaning in Saint Louis.

1. Food Spatters and Grease Stains

You may have stared at the marinara splatter on your stove so long that you’ve started to ignore it, but your guests will not. Cooking leaves behind grease and stains on appliances, walls, and counters.

Fix it: Wipe down your stovetop, range hood, backsplash, and oven door with a degreaser. For stubborn stains, a paste of baking soda and water works wonders. (Avoid vinegar here—it doesn’t effectively break down grease.)

2. Dirty Refrigerators

Ever opened a fridge at a friend's house and seen spills, expired food, or a lingering odor? Your guests will notice too.

Fix it: Before guests arrive, remove expired items, wipe down shelves with a mixture of warm water and mild dish soap, and place an open box of baking soda inside to neutralize odors. Don’t forget to clean the fridge handle and door!

3. Grimy Light Switches and Doorknobs

These high-touch areas collect grime and germs but are often forgotten during regular cleaning.

Fix it: Wipe light switch plates, doorknobs, and cabinet handles with a disinfectant wipe or a cloth dampened with rubbing alcohol. This not only removes dirt but also kills germs.

4. Dusty Surfaces and Cobwebs

Your guests will notice dust buildup on ceiling fans, baseboards, and forgotten shelves.

Fix it: Dust surfaces with a microfiber cloth, which traps dust rather than spreading it around. Use a vacuum with an extension wand to reach ceiling corners and fan blades.

5. Lingering Pet Odors and Fur

Even if you love your pets, their scent may be overwhelming to guests—especially those with allergies.

Fix it: Vacuum carpets, furniture, and pet beds thoroughly. Wash pet bedding regularly and use an enzymatic cleaner to break down odors. Consider an air purifier for long-term odor control.

6. Bathroom Horror Stories

A dirty bathroom is one of the biggest guest turn-offs. Mildew in the shower, toothpaste in the sink, and a questionable toilet ring? No, thank you!

Fix it: Scrub the toilet (including under the rim), wipe down mirrors and countertops, and wash bath mats and towels. Keep an air freshener in the bathroom and make sure soap and clean towels are readily available.

7. Musty or Stale Odors

Your home may smell fine to you, but your guests might pick up on odors from cooking, pets, or stale air.

Fix it: Open windows to air out your home. Simmer lemon slices, cinnamon sticks, and cloves on the stove for a fresh scent, or use essential oil diffusers. Avoid heavy artificial sprays that can be overwhelming.

8. Clutter Everywhere

A cluttered home can make guests feel uneasy and unwelcome.

Fix it: Declutter common areas by putting away items that don’t belong, organizing shoes and coats, and clearing surfaces like coffee tables and kitchen counters.

9. Stained or Smelly Upholstery

Your couch might be your cozy retreat, but to guests, it could be a collection of stains and odors.

Fix it: Vacuum furniture, use upholstery cleaner for stains, and spray with a fabric refresher. If possible, steam clean your couch before a big gathering.

10. A Lack of Clean Towels and Basic Amenities

Guests shouldn’t have to ask for hand towels or wonder if their bed linens are fresh.

Fix it: Provide clean towels, washcloths, and fresh bedding. Stock the guest bathroom with extra toiletries like soap, shampoo, and toothpaste in case they forgot something.
