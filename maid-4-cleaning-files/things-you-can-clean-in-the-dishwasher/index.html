10 Surprising Things You Can Clean in the Dishwasher

Your dishwasher isn’t just for plates and cups—it can also sanitize many household items you probably never considered. Maid 4 Cleaning, your trusted St. Louis cleaning service, is here to share these 10 surprising items that can be safely cleaned in your dishwasher. Benefits of Using Your Dishwasher More Often

  • Efficient Multitasking: Clean multiple items simultaneously, freeing up time for other tasks.

  • Time-Saving: Reduce manual scrubbing and effortlessly sanitize items.

  • Superior Disinfection: Dishwashers heat water up to 160 degrees Fahrenheit, providing thorough sanitization without harsh chemicals.

10 Unexpected Items You Can Clean in the Dishwasher

1. Cleaning Tools

Keep your sponges, scrub brushes, and mop heads hygienic by placing them on the dishwasher's top rack. Use a cup of white vinegar instead of detergent to disinfect effectively.

2. Silicone Oven Mitts

Regularly sanitize your silicone oven mitts in the dishwasher to remove bacteria and food residue. Fabric mitts should be washed separately in your laundry.

3. Makeup Brushes

Makeup brushes accumulate dirt and oils. Quickly clean them by placing brushes on the top rack to preserve their shape and sanitize thoroughly.

4. Bath Toys

Bath toys attract mold and mildew if not sanitized regularly. Toss them into the dishwasher monthly to keep them clean and safe for children.

5. Pet Bowls and Toys

Pet dishes and toys can harbor harmful germs. Sanitize ceramic, metal, and plastic bowls on a separate cycle in your dishwasher for optimal hygiene.

6. Sports Gear

Sports equipment like mouth guards, caps, and protective pads become breeding grounds for bacteria. Place them in a mesh bag and run them through your dishwasher weekly.

7. Gardening Tools

Gardening tools carry bacteria, fungi, and pesticides. Thoroughly sanitize small gardening tools in the dishwasher to protect your plants and yourself.

8. Hairbrushes and Combs

Plastic combs and hairbrushes can be safely cleaned on your dishwasher's top or bottom rack. Avoid putting natural-bristle or wooden-handled brushes in the dishwasher.

9. Exhaust Fan Covers and Filters

Kitchen exhaust fans collect grease and dirt. Wash removable filters and covers in the dishwasher at least once a month to maintain cleanliness and effectiveness.

10. Light Fixture Covers

Dusty and bug-filled light fixtures diminish your home’s appeal. Place glass covers and fixtures in your dishwasher annually for a sparkling clean.
