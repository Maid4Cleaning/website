Shower Cleaning Basics: How to Keep Your Bathroom Fresh and Spotless

Let’s be honest—cleaning the shower is one of those chores many of us delay until it’s begging for attention. Soap scum, mildew, clogged drains... it’s not glamorous, but it is essential for a healthy home. Luckily, your Saint Louis neighbors at Maid 4 Cleaning have gathered our best tips to help you make this task faster, easier, and way more effective.

So roll up those sleeves—here’s how to clean your shower the right way.

1. Shower Doors: Keep the Glass Gleaming

Hard water stains and soap scum can cloud your glass doors quickly. The secret? Prevention is key. After every shower, use a microfiber cloth or squeegee to wipe down the doors. This quick step prevents buildup and keeps your bathroom looking polished.

For a deeper clean, make your own streak-free solution with:

  • ¾ cup water

  • ¼ cup white vinegar (do not use on stone)

  • Microwave the mix for 45 seconds, pour into a spray bottle, and spritz generously

  • Let sit for 3–5 minutes, then wipe with a soft microfiber cloth

Bonus tip: Use a toothbrush dipped in the same solution to clean gunky door tracks.

2. The Showerhead: Restore Strong, Clean Flow

That fixture above your head? It builds up mineral deposits over time, especially in Saint Louis where hard water is common.

Here’s a quick fix:

  • Fill a sandwich bag with equal parts white vinegar and water

  • Attach it over the showerhead with a rubber band

  • Let it soak for a few hours (or overnight)

  • Remove and scrub gently with an old toothbrush

This helps restore water pressure and reduces bacteria buildup.

3. Unclog the Shower Drain: Keep the Water Flowing

Nothing ruins a relaxing shower faster than standing in ankle-deep water. Prevent clogs by:

  • Installing a hair catcher (seriously, a game-changer)

  • Cleaning the drain stopper weekly

  • Avoiding oily products like moisturizers or in-shower lotions that coat pipes

Already clogged?

  • Start with a kettle of boiling water

  • If it’s still slow, try a plumber’s snake or call a professional—over-the-counter drain cleaners can corrode your pipes over time

4. Mildew: Beat the Bathroom Fungus

The warm, humid environment in your shower is mildew’s paradise. Fight back by:

  • Wiping down walls and corners with a microfiber towel daily

  • Keeping the bathroom fan running during and after showers

  • Spraying tile with a mildew-fighting solution weekly

If you have grout lines, grab a toothbrush and a grout-safe cleaner. Skip vinegar here if your shower includes natural stone. Consider switching to a mild hydrogen peroxide-based product for safe, powerful results.

5. Cleaning for Your Shower Type

Bathtub/Shower Combos:

  • Focus on both the tub and vertical surfaces

  • Use a non-abrasive cleaner for soap scum

  • Regularly scrub grout to prevent mildew stains

Stand-alone Showers:

  • Spray glass walls and doors with a daily cleaner

  • Scrub tile or acrylic surfaces weekly

  • Don’t forget the corners—those are mildew’s favorite hiding spots

If your shower includes stone, always use products specifically labeled “safe for stone” to avoid etching or discoloration.

A Few Pro Tips From the Pros at Maid 4 Cleaning:

  • Microfiber cloths beat paper towels every time—they’re more absorbent, reusable, and less likely to scratch surfaces.

  • Ventilation is your best friend. Keep air moving with a fan or crack a window to prevent mold.

  • Declutter your products. Less stuff in the shower means fewer surfaces to clean and less chance of grime buildup under bottles.
