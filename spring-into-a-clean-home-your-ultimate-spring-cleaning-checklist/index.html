🌟 Why Spring Cleaning Matters

Spring cleaning is more than a tradition—it's a reset button. Decluttering and deep-cleaning eliminate allergens, dust, and winter’s grime. Studies show that a tidy space can reduce stress and improve well-being​.

And with Saint Louis weather getting warmer, now's the time to open those windows and let the fresh air (and freshness) in!

🏡 Whole-Home Checklist: Room-by-Room Deep Clean

✅ First: Declutter Before You Deep Clean

Use the "three-bin" method: Keep, Donate, Toss. Clutter hides dirt and makes cleaning harder. Start one room at a time and don't overwhelm yourself.

✨ Living Areas

  • Dust from top to bottom: Ceiling fans, vents, baseboards, and corners.
  • Vacuum upholstery: Use crevice tools for under cushions.
  • Wash throws and curtains: They trap allergens more than you think.
  • Polish wood furniture: Use a product suited for your wood type.

🍳 Kitchen

  • Degrease appliances: Use enzyme-based cleaners—skip the vinegar and baking soda combo, which cancels itself out chemically​.
  • Empty and wipe cabinets: Line shelves with easy-clean mats.
  • Deep-clean the fridge: Remove drawers and wash with dish soap and water. Avoid harsh chemicals near food storage.
  • Behind and under appliances: Use a slim duster or vacuum hose.

🛏 Bedrooms

  • Rotate and vacuum mattresses: Sprinkle with baking soda, let sit, then vacuum for freshness.
  • Wash bedding and pillows: Use hot water if safe for the fabric.
  • Organize closets: Store winter wear and donate what you didn’t use.
  • Dust decor and electronics: Use microfiber cloths to prevent scratches.

🚿 Bathrooms

  • Scrub grout: Use a specialty grout brush with an alkaline cleaner. Vinegar is acidic and can damage some tile surfaces​.
  • Clean showerheads: Soak in vinegar alone—don’t mix with baking soda.
  • Disinfect high-touch areas: Faucets, handles, light switches.
  • Purge old products: Check medicine and makeup expiration dates.

🌼 Bonus Tips from the Pros at Maid 4 Cleaning

  • Don’t overdo it: Clean in manageable sessions to avoid burnout.
  • Use microfiber cloths: They remove 99% of bacteria with just water​.
  • Go room-by-room: Create a checklist and celebrate your progress!
