The Best Grout Cleaning Tools for a Spotless Home

Grout that’s turning grey can make even the cleanest bathroom or kitchen look neglected. If you're ready to refresh your grout and bring back its original brightness, the right tools and cleaning solutions are essential.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know that Saint Louis homeowners want effective, easy solutions for keeping their homes looking their best. Whether you prefer natural DIY cleaners or professional-strength options, these grout-cleaning tools and techniques will help you achieve sparkling results.

Essential Grout-Cleaning Tools

Most grout-cleaning tools are inexpensive and easy to find at local Saint Louis hardware stores. Here are the most effective ones:

  • Grout-Cleaning Brush: A stiff-bristled brush designed specifically for grout lines is the best tool for deep scrubbing.

  • Old Toothbrush: If you don’t have a grout brush, an old toothbrush can do the trick—though an electric toothbrush can make the job faster and easier.

  • Steam Cleaner: Steamers are one of the most effective and eco-friendly ways to clean grout. They break down dirt and bacteria without chemicals—ideal for families and pet owners.

  • Plastic Scraper or Chisel Tool: These small hand tools help remove built-up grime without damaging tiles.

DIY Grout-Cleaning Solutions

Homemade grout cleaners can be just as effective as store-bought options—plus, they’re safe and budget-friendly. Try these simple solutions:

  1. Baking Soda & Water: Create a paste with two parts baking soda and one part water. Apply to grout, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then scrub.

  2. Baking Soda & Dish Soap: Add a teaspoon of dish soap to your baking soda paste for extra grease-cutting power.

  3. Vinegar & Water (For Non-Stone Surfaces): Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle, apply to grout, let sit for 30 minutes, then scrub.

Note: Avoid vinegar-based cleaners on natural stone tiles, as they can cause damage.

How to Clean Grout Like a Pro

Follow these easy steps for best results:

  1. Apply your chosen grout cleaner (DIY or store-bought) and let it sit for 10-30 minutes, depending on the solution.

  2. Scrub using a grout brush or old toothbrush, focusing on tough stains.

  3. Rinse with warm water and wipe away any residue.

  4. For stubborn stains, repeat the process or try a steam cleaner to lift deep-set dirt.

  5. Seal your grout once it's dry to protect it from future stains and moisture buildup.
