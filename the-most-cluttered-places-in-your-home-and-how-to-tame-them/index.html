The Most Cluttered Places in Your Home—and How to Tame Them

Clutter has a sneaky way of accumulating before we even notice. One day, your entryway is clear, and the next, you’re tripping over shoes and scrambling to find your keys. Keeping a tidy home starts with identifying clutter-prone areas and implementing smart solutions to maintain order.

At Maid 4 Cleaning in Saint Louis, we understand how overwhelming clutter can feel. That’s why we’ve compiled a list of the five most cluttered spaces in your home—plus practical, stress-free ways to keep them organized.

1. Declutter Your Entryway

Your home’s entryway is a hotspot for clutter, collecting everything from shoes and coats to mail and keys. A chaotic entry can make your home feel instantly disorganized.

How to Fix It:

  • Install a shoe rack or cubby system to store frequently worn footwear.
  • Use wall-mounted hooks or a coat rack for jackets, bags, and purses.
  • Set up a small table or wall-mounted organizer for keys, wallets, and mail.
  • Add a storage bench to corral loose items while providing a seat for putting on shoes.

2. Tidy Up the Kitchen Countertops

The kitchen is the heart of the home, but cluttered countertops can make cooking and cleaning stressful.

How to Fix It:

  • Designate a spot for mail and paperwork with a basket or wall-mounted sorter.
  • Store small appliances you don’t use daily in cabinets to free up space.
  • Implement a “one-touch rule” for dishes—wash or load them into the dishwasher immediately after use.
  • Use drawer dividers and lazy Susans to keep kitchen utensils and pantry items organized.

3. Organize Your Home Office or Desk

Mail, paperwork, and random odds and ends tend to accumulate on desks, making it hard to focus.

How to Fix It:

  • Use stackable trays labeled for bills, important documents, and miscellaneous papers.
  • Store office supplies in containers or drawer organizers.
  • Shred or recycle unnecessary paperwork weekly to prevent buildup.
  • Implement a digital filing system to reduce physical clutter.

4. Simplify Your Closets

Closets often become dumping grounds for clothes, shoes, and accessories we no longer use. A disorganized closet can make getting dressed a frustrating experience.

How to Fix It:

  • Declutter seasonally—if you haven’t worn it in a year, donate or discard it.
  • Use slim, non-slip hangers to maximize space and keep clothes from falling.
  • Store seasonal items in labeled bins on higher shelves.
  • Install hooks on closet doors for scarves, belts, and jewelry.

5. Take Control of Your Garage

The garage is often a storage nightmare, filled with tools, sports equipment, and items we’re hesitant to part with.

How to Fix It:

  • Categorize items by purpose—sports gear, tools, seasonal decor, etc.
  • Install wall-mounted shelving and pegboards for vertical storage.
  • Use clear bins with labels to store infrequently used items.
  • Schedule a bi-annual purge to clear out unnecessary clutter.

Create a Home That Sparks Joy

A clutter-free home isn’t just about aesthetics—it’s about creating a peaceful environment where you can relax and recharge.
