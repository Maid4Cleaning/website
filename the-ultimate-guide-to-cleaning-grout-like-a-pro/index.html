The Ultimate Guide to Cleaning Grout Like a Pro

Are your grout lines looking dull, stained, or even moldy? Over time, grout absorbs dirt, soap scum, and moisture, leading to discoloration and mildew buildup. Whether it’s your kitchen backsplash, bathroom tiles, or floors, properly cleaning grout can restore its original color and extend its lifespan.

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know that Saint Louis homeowners want a hassle-free way to keep their tile floors and surfaces spotless. Here’s our step-by-step guide to cleaning grout effectively—and when to call in the pros!

Why Does Grout Get So Dirty?

Grout is a porous material, meaning it absorbs dirt, grease, and moisture over time. In high-humidity areas like bathrooms, mildew and mold can thrive in unsealed grout. Regular maintenance and sealing can help prevent long-term damage.

Best Methods for Cleaning Grout

1. Routine Grout Cleaning (For Light Dirt & Stains)

🧽 What You’ll Need:

  • Warm water
  • Mild dish soap
  • A soft-bristle brush or old toothbrush

✔️ How To Do It:

  1. Mix warm water with a drop of dish soap in a spray bottle.
  2. Spray onto the grout and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  3. Scrub gently with a grout brush or toothbrush.
  4. Wipe clean with a damp cloth.

🔹 Best for: Regular upkeep in low-traffic areas.

2. Deep Cleaning Old or Stained Grout

🧼 What You’ll Need:

  • Heavy-duty cleaner (such as a wax stripper)
  • Baking soda & vinegar (or hydrogen peroxide for white grout)
  • A stiff grout brush

✔️ How To Do It:

  1. Apply a grout cleaner or a paste of baking soda and water.
  2. Scrub in circular motions using a stiff grout brush.
  3. If stains persist, spray vinegar (for natural stone surfaces, use hydrogen peroxide instead).
  4. Let sit for 5–10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

💡 Pro Tip: Vinegar can weaken grout over time—so use sparingly!

3. Removing Mold & Mildew From Grout

🚿 What You’ll Need:

  • A 1:5 mixture of chlorine bleach and water
  • A grout brush
  • Protective gloves & a mask

⚠️ Important: Never mix bleach with ammonia-based products—it creates toxic fumes.

✔️ How To Do It:

  1. Dip the grout brush into the bleach solution.
  2. Scrub the affected grout lines.
  3. Let sit for 10 minutes, then rinse with warm water.
  4. Ensure the area is well-ventilated during and after cleaning.

📌 Best for: Showers, tubs, and high-humidity areas.

4. Cleaning Colored Grout (Without Fading)

🎨 What You’ll Need:

  • Heavy-duty cleaner (NO bleach!)
  • A soft-bristle brush
  • Masonry sealer (optional)

✔️ How To Do It:

  1. Use a grout cleaner that’s safe for colored grout (no bleach-based products).
  2. Scrub gently with a soft-bristle brush.
  3. Rinse and let dry completely.
  4. Apply a masonry sealer to preserve color and prevent future stains.

💡 Best for: Decorative tile and accent walls.

The Final Step: Sealing Your Grout

Once your grout is clean and completely dry, applying a masonry sealer can help prevent future stains and make cleaning easier. We recommend sealing grout every 6–12 months, especially in high-traffic areas like kitchens and bathrooms.

FAQs About Grout Cleaning

❓ How often should I clean my grout?
✔️ For best results, lightly clean your grout once a week and do a deep clean every 2–3 months.

❓ Can I use a steam cleaner on grout?
✔️ Yes! Steam cleaning is great for removing grime without harsh chemicals. However, avoid it on cracked grout to prevent water damage.

❓ Does bleach damage grout?
✔️ Bleach is safe for white grout in moderation, but overuse can weaken and erode grout lines. Always rinse thoroughly.
