The Ultimate Guide to Cleaning Your Microwave

Microwaves are a kitchen essential, but let's be honest—cleaning them feels like a chore. Weeks or even months of splattered sauces and forgotten leftovers can leave your microwave looking like a culinary crime scene.

But don’t worry! Maid 4 Cleaning in Saint Louis, MO, is here to help. This guide will walk you through the most effective microwave cleaning methods, whether you're looking for a quick freshen-up or tackling years of stubborn grime. Plus, we’ll share pro tips to keep your microwave cleaner for longer—saving you time and effort!

Safety First!

Before you start, always unplug your microwave. This simple step ensures safety and prevents electrical mishaps.

Method 1: Routine Steam Cleaning (For Light Spills & Everyday Cleaning)

What You'll Need:

✔ A microwave-safe bowl or cup
✔ 1 cup of water
✔ 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar (for freshening & disinfecting)
✔ Microfiber cloth or paper towels

Steps:

  1. Prep the Steam: Add 1 cup of water to your microwave-safe bowl. Squeeze in fresh lemon juice or add white vinegar for its natural degreasing power.

  2. Heat It Up: Place the bowl in the microwave and run it on high for 3-5 minutes. This will create steam that loosens grime and food splatters.

  3. Let It Sit: Leave the microwave door closed for 5 minutes to allow steam to soften any debris.

  4. Wipe Clean: Carefully remove the bowl (it will be hot!) and wipe the interior with a damp microfiber cloth or paper towels.

  5. Clean the Turntable: Remove and wash it in hot, soapy water. If dishwasher-safe, simply pop it in the dishwasher.

  6. Final Touch: Wipe the interior with a dry microfiber cloth to absorb any moisture.

Pro Tip:

Lemon juice and vinegar break down grease and kill bacteria, making this method ideal for regular maintenance!

Method 2: Baking Soda Deep Clean (For Stubborn, Baked-On Grime)

If your microwave looks like it’s been through a food fight, you need baking soda’s powerful, yet gentle scrubbing ability.

What You'll Need:

✔ 4 tablespoons of baking soda
✔ 1 quart of warm water
✔ Microfiber cloth or non-abrasive sponge

Steps:

  1. Create a Paste: Mix baking soda and water to form a thick paste.

  2. Apply & Scrub: Remove the turntable and apply the paste to the interior walls and door. Let it sit for 5 minutes.

  3. Wipe It Down: Use a damp microfiber cloth to gently scrub and remove stains.

  4. Rinse & Dry: Wipe the interior with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue, then dry with a paper towel.

Pro Tip:

Baking soda is a natural deodorizer, so it helps eliminate lingering food odors!

How to Clean the Outside of Your Microwave

Don’t forget about the exterior! A clean microwave enhances your kitchen’s appearance and prevents the spread of bacteria.

What You'll Need:

✔ All-purpose cleaner or diluted white vinegar
✔ Microfiber cloth

Steps:

  1. Spray & Wipe: Lightly spray a microfiber cloth with vinegar or an all-purpose cleaner (don’t spray directly onto the microwave!).

  2. Pay Extra Attention to High-Touch Areas: Focus on handles, buttons, and the display panel.

  3. Buff & Dry: Use a clean, dry cloth to remove streaks and leave a polished finish.

Pro Tip:

For stainless steel microwaves, use a stainless steel cleaner to prevent streaking!

Keep Your Microwave Clean Longer with These Tips

✔ Wipe spills immediately – Fresh spills are much easier to clean!
✔ Use microwave-safe covers – Prevent splatters by covering food with a microwave-safe lid or paper towel.
✔ Don’t overheat food – Overcooking can cause food to burst, creating a mess.
✔ Do a weekly wipe-down – A quick wipe-down every few days prevents buildup.
