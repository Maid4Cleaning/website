The Ultimate Guide to Eliminating Dog Hair and Pet Odor from Your Home

There’s nothing quite like the joy of having a furry friend. Whether it’s the enthusiastic tail wags when you walk through the door or the unconditional love they provide, dogs truly become part of the family. However, along with all that love comes the challenge of keeping your home free from pet hair, odors, and the occasional accident.

Maintaining a fresh, clean home while living with pets doesn’t have to be a losing battle. With the right approach, you can enjoy the company of your pup without sacrificing cleanliness. Here are our top expert-backed tips to help you tackle pet hair, neutralize odors, and keep your home looking and smelling fresh.

1. Control Shedding with Daily Grooming

One of the most effective ways to reduce pet hair in your home is by stopping it at the source. Regular grooming is essential, especially during shedding season.

  • Brush your dog daily using a de-shedding tool or grooming glove to remove loose fur before it lands on your furniture and floors.
  • Invest in a high-quality pet vacuum with a HEPA filter to trap pet dander and fine hairs.
  • Use a lint roller or a damp rubber glove to quickly pick up fur from upholstery and clothing.
  • Consider professional grooming every few weeks to help keep shedding under control.

Pro Tip: A pet-friendly microfiber cloth is an excellent tool for picking up loose fur from furniture and hard surfaces.

2. The Best Way to Remove Pet Odors

Dog odors can linger, especially in carpets, furniture, and bedding. Instead of masking odors, eliminate them at the source:

  • Wash your pet’s bedding and toys weekly using a pet-safe detergent.
  • Sprinkle baking soda on carpets before vacuuming to absorb odors naturally.
  • Use enzyme-based cleaners for accidents—these break down organic messes and fully eliminate lingering smells.
  • Regularly bathe your dog using a gentle, deodorizing shampoo.

Pro Tip: Avoid mixing vinegar and baking soda for cleaning pet messes—it may create fizzing action but is not effective at breaking down stains or odors chemically.

3. Pet Accidents? Clean Them Properly

Accidents happen, but how you clean them makes all the difference in preventing repeat offenses. Follow these steps for effective cleanup:

  1. Act fast – The sooner you clean up, the easier it will be to remove the stain and odor.
  2. Blot, don’t rub – Use paper towels or coffee filters to soak up as much liquid as possible without spreading the mess.
  3. Apply an enzymatic cleaner – These cleaners break down pet urine on a molecular level and prevent your pet from marking the same spot again.
  4. Cover with plastic wrap – This allows the cleaner to work more effectively by preventing evaporation.
  5. Deep clean carpets and upholstery regularly – Consider professional carpet cleaning services to keep pet odors at bay.

Pro Tip: Place your pet’s food or water dish near accident-prone areas—dogs avoid soiling places where they eat.

4. Improve Air Quality in a Pet-Friendly Home

Pet hair and dander don’t just settle on surfaces—they also affect the air quality inside your home.

  • Use an air purifier with a HEPA filter to remove pet dander and allergens from the air.
  • Open windows regularly to allow fresh air to circulate.
  • Change your HVAC filters frequently to prevent buildup of hair and dust.
  • Opt for odor-neutralizing candles or diffusers designed for pet homes.

Pro Tip: Houseplants like spider plants and bamboo palms help naturally purify indoor air and absorb pet odors.

5. Protect Your Floors and Furniture

Your pet loves to cuddle on the couch or sprawl out on the carpet, but that doesn’t mean your furniture and flooring should suffer.

  • Use washable slipcovers on sofas and chairs for easy maintenance.
  • Invest in pet-friendly rugs that are stain-resistant and machine-washable.
  • For hardwood floors, use a microfiber mop to capture fur and dust efficiently.
  • Keep paw wipes near the door to clean your dog’s feet before they enter the house.

Pro Tip: If your dog has a favorite napping spot, cover it with a washable blanket to make cleanup easier.
