The Ultimate January Cleaning Checklist

Start Fresh This New Year with Maid 4 Cleaning in Saint Louis

January brings a fresh start—and with it, the perfect excuse to hit reset on your home. After the holiday hustle and winter weather, your space might be feeling more chaotic than cozy. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we’re here to help you get your home sparkling and stress-free with our local, professional maid services in Saint Louis.

1. Clear Out Holiday Clutter (and Hidden Glitter)

Start by packing up decorations and storing them in labeled bins. Don’t forget those sneaky spots—under furniture, behind TVs, or under rugs—for stray pine needles or glitter. Use microfiber cloths to dust surfaces (they’re more effective than cotton and use less product). As Ann Russell wisely says, “You don't need to dismantle your toilet to have a clean home—you just need routine.”

Pro Tip: While cleaning, sort out what you didn’t use this season and donate it now instead of storing it again.

2. Restore Muddy Entryways

Winter in Saint Louis means snow, slush, and salt—trackers of mess. Here's how to reclaim your entryway:

  • Shake it out: Take rugs outdoors for a good shake or wash.
  • Scrub away salt: Use warm water with a mild detergent—not vinegar!—to safely lift salt stains from wood or tile floors.
  • Organize gear: Create a station for boots, coats, and scarves using trays and hooks. Martha Stewart suggests labeling bins or baskets for each family member to cut down on morning chaos.

3. Refresh Carpets & Upholstery

Your furniture and floors have been through a lot—muddy paws, holiday parties, and all. Vacuum high-traffic areas thoroughly and treat stains with appropriate products (never mix vinegar and baking soda; it neutralizes their power!). For deep stains or a true refresh, call us at Maid 4 Cleaning. Our pros can bring your carpet back to life.

4. Dust Vents for Better Air Quality

With heaters running nonstop, dust buildup can mess with your indoor air.

  • Remove vent covers and vacuum inside.
  • Use a microfiber cloth dampened with soapy water to wipe covers..

Cleaner vents = cleaner lungs. Especially important if you have pets or allergies!

5. Deep Clean the Kitchen (Beyond the Surface)

After all the baking and holiday meals, your kitchen needs love:

  • Oven & stovetop: Use a baking soda paste (skip the vinegar!) for baked-on messes. Let it sit before scrubbing.
  • Fridge: Toss expired food, wipe down shelves, and vacuum fridge coils to help it run efficiently.
  • Sink & counters: Use a disinfecting spray—soap alone cleans but doesn’t disinfect.

6. Wash Windows (Inside is Enough)

Winter light is precious. Clean interior windows with a streak-free glass cleaner. Don’t forget sills and tracks—these gather more dirt than you'd think. For pro-level shine without streaks, Maid 4 Cleaning offers detailed window services that brighten any room.

7. Don’t Forget the Forgotten

Dust loves corners, baseboards, ceiling fans, and behind appliances. Tackle these now so you don’t dread spring cleaning later.

  • Use an extendable duster for ceilings.
  • Wipe baseboards with a damp microfiber cloth.
  • Check smoke detectors while you’re up there!

8. Create Your New Cleaning Routine

Now that your home is sparkling, keep it that way:

  • Daily: Quick 5-minute declutter.
  • Weekly: Assign vacuuming, dusting, and bathroom wipes.
  • Monthly: Clean behind appliances and check pantry dates.
