To Squeegee or Not to Squeegee? What Homeowners Should Know

At Maid 4 Cleaning, we believe it’s the little habits that keep your home feeling fresh, clean, and welcoming. One of the simplest tools that can make a big impact in your bathroom is the humble squeegee. Yes the same tool you might see used on storefront windows is actually a secret weapon for sparkling showers!

If you’ve ever asked yourself, “Do I really need to squeegee after every shower?” we’ve got answers. Let’s dive into the pros (and very few cons) of adding a shower squeegee to your routine.

Why the Shower Squeegee is a Game-Changer

✅ Pro #1: It Saves Time and Energy
Unlike using a towel that gets soaked quickly, a squeegee cuts through moisture efficiently in a few quick swipes. No more foggy glass or streaky tile! Just run the blade top to bottom on shower doors and walls, and you're done in under a minute.

✅ Pro #2: It Prevents Build-Up
Soap scum and hard water spots are no joke in St. Louis our water tends to run hard, which leaves behind calcium and lime deposits. A daily swipe with a squeegee removes excess water, reducing the chance for minerals and soap residue to settle.

✅ Pro #3: It's Low Maintenance
Most squeegees come with a suction cup or hook for easy storage right in the shower. Just rinse the blade after use and let it air dry. That’s it! Replace the blade if it starts to streak or warp.

✅ Bonus Tip: Use a Safe After-Shower Spray
After squeegeeing, lightly mist your glass and tile (not natural stone) with a diluted white vinegar solution to extend time between deep cleans. For stone surfaces like marble or granite, use a pH-neutral spray designed for stone to avoid etching.

The (Very Minor) Cons

There aren’t many, but for full transparency:

  • It’s a new habit to form, especially for households with kids or multiple shower users.

  • It may take a few tries to find your groove some people prefer vertical strokes, while others swear by the professional “fan” or “swivel” method. The good news? Both work!

Squeegee vs. Skipping It: The Difference Is Clear

According to The Science of Cleaning by Dario Bressanini, even small, consistent habits like moisture control can delay grime and microbial growth. Daily squeegeeing doesn't just make your shower look better it makes deep cleaning less frequent and far easier.
