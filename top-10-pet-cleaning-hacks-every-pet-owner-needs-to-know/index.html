Top 10 Pet Cleaning Hacks Every Pet Owner Needs to Know

Pets are a joy—until the fur, paw prints, and mystery smells start to take over. If you’re constantly cleaning up after your four-legged roommate, you’re not alone. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we know how challenging it is to keep your home fresh and fur-free in pet-loving households. That’s why we’ve compiled our top 10 professional pet cleaning hacks to help you spend less time scrubbing and more time cuddling your furry friend.

Whether you live in the Central West End, Clayton, or down by the Hill, these tried-and-true tips will help keep your space clean—and your sanity intact.

1. Rubber Gloves = Magic Fur Removers

For upholstery, nothing beats a simple pair of rubber dish gloves. Lightly dampen the gloves, then rub your furniture in sweeping motions. Pet hair clings right to the rubber like magic, no lint roller needed.

2. Groom Outside (or in a Contained Space)

Set up a grooming station in your garage, yard, or even a tiled bathroom. Brushing your pet regularly outside keeps the hair from ever hitting your floors. Less shedding inside = less cleaning.

3. Microfiber Mops Are Your Best Friend

Switch to a microfiber dry mop for hardwood and tile floors. According to Natural Cleaning Secrets and UC Davis research, microfiber can pick up 99% of pet hair and dander with just water​.

4. Invest in a HEPA Filter Vacuum

Pet dander is tiny—smaller than dust mites. Use a vacuum with a HEPA filter to actually trap the allergens instead of blowing them back out. Martha Stewart’s Homekeeping Handbook confirms that this is key for maintaining healthy indoor air​.

5. Use a Lint Roller on Lampshades, Curtains, and Pillows

Pet hair doesn’t just land on the floor. Run a lint roller across lampshades, throw pillows, and even walls (especially if you have cats).

6. Clean Paws, Cleaner House

Keep a pet-friendly doormat outside and a towel or wipes inside the door. Wipe down paws (and bellies for short pets) to prevent mud, pollen, and salt from ending up on your floors.

7. DIY Pet Odor Neutralizer

Mix a solution of distilled water, a splash of vodka or rubbing alcohol, and a few drops of lavender or citrus essential oil in a spray bottle. Mist pet beds and upholstery—this will help neutralize odors without harsh chemicals.

8. Dryer First, Then Wash

Before washing pet-covered clothing or bedding, toss it in the dryer on low heat for 10 minutes. This helps loosen and trap fur in the lint screen so it doesn't clog your washer.

9. Skip the Steam on Stains

For pet stains (especially urine), avoid steam cleaners. The heat can set the stain and smell permanently into carpet fibers. Instead, blot with enzymatic cleaners specifically designed for pet messes.
