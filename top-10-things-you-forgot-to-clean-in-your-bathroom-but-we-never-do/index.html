Top 10 Things You Forgot to Clean in Your Bathroom (But We Never Do!)

Let’s be honest—nobody’s thrilled to clean the bathroom. But it’s one of the most important areas to keep fresh and sanitary, especially since it’s a hotspot for bacteria like E. coli, staph, and salmonella. While most people focus on scrubbing the toilet, wiping the sink, and rinsing the tub, there are a few spots almost everyone forgets. At Maid 4 Cleaning, we specialize in detailed home cleaning services in Saint Louis, and we make sure no corner is overlooked.

Here are the top 10 things most people forget to clean in the bathroom (but we never miss):

1. Light Fixtures & Exhaust Fans

Steam, dust, and aerosols (hello, hairspray!) can turn your light fixtures and fan covers into sticky grime magnets. Wipe them down regularly with a microfiber cloth and mild cleaner. A vacuum with a brush attachment also works wonders on exhaust fan vents.

2. Shower Curtains & Liners

Plastic liners can trap soap scum and develop mildew, while fabric curtains absorb moisture and bacteria. Toss fabric curtains in the wash on a gentle cycle. For plastic liners, pretreat with a baking soda paste, then scrub with a dish brush and rinse thoroughly.

3. Bathroom Walls

A fine mist from flushing or steamy showers can leave residue on the walls. Spray an all-purpose cleaner and use a microfiber cloth to wipe everything down—especially behind the toilet where bacteria love to linger.

4. Light Switches & Door Handles

High-touch surfaces like light switches and door knobs are loaded with germs. Use a disinfecting wipe or a cloth sprayed with rubbing alcohol to sanitize them weekly.

5. Inside Cabinets & Drawers

From spilled cosmetics to dusty hair ties, bathroom drawers and cabinets can get messy fast. Take everything out, wipe the inside with a damp cloth, and toss expired or unused items. This is also a great time to declutter and reorganize!

6. Toilet Paper Holder & Towel Bars

These often-overlooked hardware pieces are touched daily and easily collect dust and germs. A quick wipe with disinfectant keeps them clean and shiny.

7. Toilet Base & Tank Exterior

We all clean inside the toilet—but what about underneath and behind it? Use disinfecting wipes or a cloth and spray to clean the base, bolts, and tank. Don’t forget the handle, either—it’s one of the germiest places!

8. Toothbrush Holders & Soap Dishes

These collect gunk from dripping water, toothpaste, and soap scum. Wash them with hot, soapy water weekly—or better yet, run them through the dishwasher if they’re dishwasher-safe.

9. Sink Overflow Hole

Ever notice that little hole near the top of your sink basin? That’s the overflow, and it can collect grime and mildew. Use a cotton swab and a bit of all-purpose cleaner to freshen it up.

10. Faucet Handles & Shower Controls

We touch them all the time with dirty hands, yet rarely clean them. Wipe down with a disinfecting cloth or spray to keep them sparkling and germ-free.
