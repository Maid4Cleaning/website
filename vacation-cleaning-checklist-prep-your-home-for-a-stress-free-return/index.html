Vacation Cleaning Checklist: Prep Your Home for a Stress-Free Return

When you're planning a getaway, the last thing you want is to come home to overflowing laundry baskets, stinky trash cans, or dusty surfaces. With just a little pre-trip planning, you can make sure your Saint Louis home stays fresh and welcoming while you’re away.

Here’s your ultimate Vacation Cleaning Checklist, straight from the pros at Maid 4 Cleaning!

🧺 1. Do the Laundry Early

Don't leave dirty laundry sitting for a week—or more.

  • Wash all clothes and linens. Have everyone strip their beds a couple of days before departure and wash sheets and pillowcases.

  • Dry towels properly. Even slightly damp towels can mildew in a closed bathroom. Hang them over the shower rod or towel bar to fully dry before packing up.

  • Empty the laundry hamper. Leaving behind unwashed clothes can lead to musty odors that linger.

Pro Tip: Toss a lavender-scented sachet into your linen closet before you leave. It’ll keep your freshly washed linens smelling amazing!

🍽️ 2. Kitchen Clean-Up Essentials

Nothing says "Welcome home!" like a clean, odor-free kitchen.

  • Clear the fridge. Toss anything that will spoil. Wipe down shelves with a solution of warm water and a small squirt of dish soap.

  • Wash all dishes. Run the dishwasher the night before and leave the door slightly ajar to prevent odors.

  • Sanitize the sink. Clean the basin and run a few ice cubes and lemon rinds through the garbage disposal to freshen it.

  • Take out the trash. Don’t forget bathroom and bedroom bins, too.

Pro Tip: Clean your trash can with hot water and a dash of castile soap. Let it dry fully before inserting a new liner.

🛋️ 3. Tidy Living Areas & Common Spaces

You don’t need to deep clean—but a little tidying makes a big difference.

  • Dust surfaces and vacuum rugs. Focus on high-traffic areas: living room, entryway, and hallways.

  • Declutter. Put away books, toys, and paperwork so you’re not greeted by chaos.

  • Set your robot vacuum. If you have one, program it to run every few days while you're away.

Saint Louis Smart Tip: High humidity can create musty smells. Leave closet doors cracked slightly and run your HVAC system on “auto” to circulate air.

🚽 4. Bathroom Basics

Clean bathrooms reduce the chance of mildew and odors.

  • Scrub toilets and sinks. Wipe down faucets and mirrors.

  • Leave doors open. Airflow helps prevent mold growth.

  • Swap out bathmats. Wash and dry them ahead of your trip.

Pro Tip: A small bowl of baking soda left on the counter (out of reach of pets!) can help absorb humidity and odors.

🚗 5. Clean the Car (Yes, Really!)

If you're road-tripping, a tidy car makes a big difference.

  • Vacuum the seats and trunk. Clear out old snacks, cups, and wrappers.

  • Stock your essentials. Napkins, wet wipes, and a small trash bag are road trip lifesavers.

  • Leave space for souvenirs. Empty the trunk to make room for luggage and shopping bags.

🧳 6. Final Vacation Prep

Don't forget the trip itself!

  • Pack ahead. Don’t leave it all until the night before—reduce stress and make sure everything’s clean and ready.

  • Pet and plant care. Arrange for a neighbor or sitter and leave clear instructions.

  • Set timers. Smart lights and thermostats can help maintain home security and climate control while you're gone.
