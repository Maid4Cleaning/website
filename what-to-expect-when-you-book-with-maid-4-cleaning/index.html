What to Expect When You Book with Maid 4 Cleaning

Be Swept Away by a Cleaner, Happier Home

If you're new to professional cleaning services, you might be wondering: Should I tidy up before the cleaners arrive? What do they actually do? At Maid 4 Cleaning, we’re here to take the stress out of cleaning—so you can get your time (and sanity) back.

Here’s exactly what to expect from our visits—and how a little prep on your part can help you get the most out of every cleaning.

What We Take Care Of

Our team is trained to clean efficiently and thoroughly, using proven techniques and top-tier supplies. With every visit, we aim to leave your Saint Louis home sparkling and refreshed.

Standard Services Include:

  • Scrubbing and sanitizing sinks, tubs, toilets, and counters

  • Dusting furniture, decor, and baseboards

  • Cleaning appliance exteriors and kitchen surfaces

  • Sweeping, vacuuming, and mopping all floors

  • Changing bed linens (just leave fresh ones out for us)

Need extras like inside oven cleaning or help before a move? Just ask! We customize every cleaning.

How to Prepare for Your Cleaning

You don’t have to clean before we show up (that’s what we’re here for!), but a few small actions can make a big difference:

1. Clear Floors and Counters

Remove dishes, clutter, laundry, or paperwork from high-traffic areas so we can clean more deeply and quickly. Less clutter = better results.

2. Set Out Fresh Linens

If we’re changing beds, place clean sheets on each mattress. This keeps things simple and ensures the right linens go on the right beds.

3. Put Away Valuables

Store personal or sensitive items like documents, jewelry, or medication in a safe spot for peace of mind.

4. Leave Notes or Special Instructions

If you have areas you want us to focus on—or avoid—let us know! A short list or note helps us personalize your cleaning, especially if you're not home during the visit.

Expert Cleaning Tips for Maintaining Your Home

Want to stretch the time between cleanings? Here are a few secrets we swear by:

  • Use microfiber cloths: They trap dirt and bacteria better than cotton or paper and reduce the need for chemicals.

  • Keep a cleaning caddy: Stock a bin with essentials like all-purpose spray, a glass cleaner, gloves, and cloths. It saves time when you need to spot-clean.

  • Work top-to-bottom: Dust settles downward, so always clean higher surfaces (like shelves) before tackling the floor.

  • Don’t overdo products: More spray doesn’t equal more clean—just streaks and residue. Follow label directions for best results.

  • Declutter daily: Spend 5 minutes picking up items in high-traffic areas like the entryway, kitchen, and bathrooms. It helps your next cleaning go further.
